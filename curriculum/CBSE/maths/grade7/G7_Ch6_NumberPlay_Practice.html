Is 48 even or odd? How do you know?
Easy
Answer

Answer: Even

Method: Last-Digit Rule
Step 1: Look at the last digit of 48 — it is 8.
Step 2: 8 is even (divisible by 2), so 48 is even.
Key Rule: A number is even if its last digit is 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.

Key Rule: A number is odd if its last digit is 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.

Explain why a number can NEVER be both even and odd simultaneously.
Hard
Answer

Answer: Because remainder when dividing by 2 is either 0 (even) or 1 (odd) — never both.

Method: Division Algorithm
For any integer n: n = 2q + r where r ∈ {0, 1}. The remainder r is unique — it cannot be both 0 and 1 at the same time.
So every integer is exactly one of even (r=0) or odd (r=1).
Key Rule: Parity is determined by a unique remainder — it is mutually exclusive.
