Set A Identifying Axes and Origin

What is the name of the horizontal number line in the Cartesian plane?
Show Answer

It is called the x-axis (also called the abscissa axis).

Easy
What is the name of the vertical number line in the Cartesian plane?
Show Answer

It is called the y-axis (also called the ordinate axis).

Easy
What is the point where the x-axis and y-axis intersect called, and what are its coordinates?
Show Answer

It is called the origin. Its coordinates are (0, 0).

Easy
Who invented the Cartesian coordinate system? After which mathematician is it named?
Show Answer

It was invented by René Descartes, a French mathematician. The word "Cartesian" comes from the Latin form of his name, Cartesius.

Easy
State the sign convention for points on the positive x-axis and negative x-axis.
Show Answer

Points on the positive x-axis have x > 0 (right of origin). Points on the negative x-axis have x < 0 (left of origin). In both cases, y = 0.

Easy
How many quadrants does the Cartesian plane have? Name them and describe their positions.
Show Answer

There are four quadrants: Quadrant I (top-right), Quadrant II (top-left), Quadrant III (bottom-left), Quadrant IV (bottom-right). They are numbered anticlockwise (in the standard convention) from the positive x-axis.

Easy
A point lies on the y-axis but is not the origin. What can you say about its x-coordinate?
Show Answer

Its x-coordinate is 0. Any point on the y-axis has the form (0, y) where y ≠ 0.

Medium
A point has coordinates (−5, 0). On which axis does it lie? In which direction from the origin?
Show Answer

It lies on the x-axis, specifically on the negative x-axis, 5 units to the left of the origin.

Easy
Explain the difference between the x-coordinate (abscissa) and the y-coordinate (ordinate) of a point.
Show Answer

The abscissa (x-coordinate) is the perpendicular distance of the point from the y-axis (measured along the x-axis direction). The ordinate (y-coordinate) is the perpendicular distance from the x-axis (measured along the y-axis direction).

Medium
If a point lies exactly on the x-axis at a distance of 7 units to the right of the origin, write its coordinates.
Show Answer

(7, 0)

Easy

Easy
A point lies in Quadrant III. Its x-coordinate is −3. Can its y-coordinate be +2? Explain.
Show Answer

No. In Quadrant III, both coordinates must be negative. If y = +2, the point would lie in Quadrant II.

Medium
How many quadrants have at least one negative coordinate?
Show Answer

Three quadrants: II (x negative), III (both negative), IV (y negative). Only Quadrant I has both coordinates positive.

Medium
A point is reflected across the y-axis. It was originally in Quadrant IV. In which quadrant does it land after reflection?
Show Answer

Reflecting across the y-axis changes the sign of x. Quadrant IV has (+, −). After reflection: (−, −) = Quadrant III.

Set C Axes, Plane Properties and Concepts

Can a point belong to two quadrants simultaneously? Why or why not?
Show Answer

No. Each point has a unique location. Points on the axes belong to no quadrant — the axes are the boundaries between quadrants.

Medium
The point (0, −4) lies on which axis? What is its distance from the origin?
Show Answer

It lies on the negative y-axis. Distance from origin = 4 units.

Easy
Is the point (0, 0) in any quadrant? Explain.
Show Answer

No. The origin (0, 0) is the intersection of both axes. It belongs to neither axis nor any quadrant.

Medium
Name the axes and state the quadrant for: a point with equal positive x and y coordinates (like (3, 3)).
Show Answer

Both coordinates are positive and equal, so the point lies in Quadrant I on the line y = x.

Easy
The x-axis divides the Cartesian plane into which two half-planes?
Show Answer

The x-axis divides the plane into the upper half-plane (y > 0, containing Quadrants I and II) and the lower half-plane (y < 0, containing Quadrants III and IV).

Medium
If a point (a, b) is in Quadrant II, what can you say about a + b? Give an example.
Show Answer

In Quadrant II: a < 0, b > 0. So a + b can be positive, negative, or zero depending on magnitudes. Example: (−2, 5) → a + b = 3 (positive); (−5, 2) → a + b = −3 (negative).

Hard
A point (k, k) where k > 0. In which quadrant does it always lie?
Show Answer

Quadrant I, because both coordinates are positive (k > 0).

Easy
A point (k, −k) where k > 0. In which quadrant does it lie?
Show Answer

Quadrant IV: x = k > 0 and y = −k < 0.

Medium
For what value of p does the point (p, 4) lie on the y-axis?
Show Answer

p = 0. A point lies on the y-axis when its x-coordinate is 0.

Easy
The Cartesian plane is named after Descartes. Briefly describe how he is believed to have conceived the idea.
Show Answer

Legend says Descartes observed a fly on his ceiling and realised he could describe its position using two perpendicular measurements from the corner walls — giving birth to the idea of a coordinate grid.

Medium

Set D Mixed and Application

Write the coordinates of two points, one each from Quadrant II and Quadrant IV.
Show Answer

Quadrant II: e.g. (−3, 4); Quadrant IV: e.g. (5, −2).

Easy
A point P is equidistant from both axes and lies in Quadrant III. If the distance from each axis is 6, write its coordinates.
Show Answer

Equidistant from both axes in Quadrant III: both coordinates are negative and equal in magnitude. P = (−6, −6).

Medium
In a city map modelled on a coordinate grid, Town Hall is at the origin. A school is 4 km east and 3 km north. Write its coordinates.
Show Answer

East is the positive x-direction, north is the positive y-direction. Coordinates: (4, 3).

Easy
Point A is in Quadrant I. Its reflection across the x-axis is A'. In which quadrant is A'?
Show Answer

Reflecting across the x-axis negates the y-coordinate. Quadrant I (+ , +) → (+ , −) = Quadrant IV.

Medium
If (x, y) is in Quadrant III, in which quadrant is (−x, −y)?
Show Answer

Quadrant III: x < 0, y < 0. So −x > 0 and −y > 0 → Quadrant I.

Medium
A treasure hunt map has a base camp at (0, 0). Clue 1 moves you 5 steps East and 2 steps South. Write the new coordinates and identify the quadrant.
Show Answer

East = +x, South = −y. Coordinates: (5, −2) → Quadrant IV.

Easy
If (a, b) is in Quadrant I and (c, d) is in Quadrant III, what can you say about (a+c, b+d)?
Show Answer

We cannot determine the quadrant without specific values. a > 0 and c < 0, so a + c could be positive, negative, or zero. Similarly for b + d. The result could be in any quadrant or on an axis.

Hard
How do GPS coordinates relate to the Cartesian coordinate system? Name the two measurements used in GPS.
Show Answer

GPS uses latitude (like the y-axis, measuring north/south) and longitude (like the x-axis, measuring east/west) — analogous to the y and x axes of a Cartesian plane, with the equator and prime meridian as reference lines.

Medium
A point (m, n) has m × n > 0. Which quadrants could it be in?
Show Answer

m × n > 0 means both have the same sign. Both positive → Quadrant I; both negative → Quadrant III.

Hard
Can the x-axis and y-axis of a Cartesian plane be non-perpendicular? What happens to the coordinate system if they are not?
Show Answer

By definition, the axes must be perpendicular. If they are not, the system is called an oblique coordinate system, which is valid mathematically but is not the standard Cartesian (rectangular) system and is harder to work with.

Set A Basic Plotting Recall

To plot the point (4, 3), how many units do you move along the x-axis and y-axis from the origin?
Show Answer

Move 4 units right along the x-axis, then 3 units up parallel to the y-axis.

Easy
Describe the steps to plot (−3, 5) on a coordinate grid.
Show Answer

Start at origin → move 3 units left along the x-axis (x = −3) → move 5 units up (y = 5). Mark the point.

Easy
Describe the steps to plot (−2, −6).
Show Answer

Start at origin → 2 units left (x = −2) → 6 units down (y = −6). This is in Quadrant III.

Easy
Describe the steps to plot (7, −4).
Show Answer

Start at origin → 7 units right (x = 7) → 4 units down (y = −4). This is in Quadrant IV.

Easy
Plot the origin. What are its coordinates?
Show Answer

The origin is plotted at the intersection of the two axes. Coordinates: (0, 0).

Easy
Which direction is positive on the y-axis and which is negative?
Show Answer

Upward is positive (y > 0). Downward is negative (y < 0).

Easy
A student plots (3, 5) by first going 5 units up and then 3 units right. Do they arrive at the same point? Explain.
Show Answer

Yes, they arrive at the same point. Coordinates are just perpendicular distances from the axes, so the order of movement does not matter — you end up at the same location (3, 5).

Medium
Plot the four points: A(2, 3), B(−2, 3), C(−2, −3), D(2, −3). What shape do they form?
Show Answer

The four points form a rectangle (in fact, because the sides are equal in pairs and perpendicular, it could be confirmed as a rectangle with vertices equidistant from the axes).

Medium
Why is the order of coordinates in a point important? What happens if (3, 5) is written as (5, 3)?
Show Answer

Order matters because coordinates are an ordered pair. (3, 5) means x = 3, y = 5; (5, 3) means x = 5, y = 3. These are different points unless both values are equal.

Medium
Mark and label the five points: O(0,0), P(4,0), Q(0,3), R(−4,0), S(0,−3). What figures do PQRS, PRQ, and QRS each describe?
Show Answer

P, Q, R, S lie on the axes symmetrically. Triangle PQR and similar triangles can be drawn. OPQS forms a right-angled set. The four points P, Q, R, S form a rhombus (a square rotated 45°) with diagonals along the axes of length 8 and 6.

(−5, 0) — lies on the negative x-axis.

Plot the vertices of a triangle: A(0, 4), B(−3, −2), C(3, −2). Describe the type of triangle.

A is directly above the midpoint of BC. BC is horizontal. Since B and C are symmetric about the y-axis, AB = AC. This is an isosceles triangle.

Medium
A point P is 4 units from the y-axis (to the right) and 3 units from the x-axis (below). Write P's coordinates.

Right of y-axis → x = +4; below x-axis → y = −3. P = (4, −3).

Easy
List 3 points, all with the same x-coordinate of −2 but different y-coordinates. What do they have in common geometrically?
Show Answer

E.g., (−2, 1), (−2, 4), (−2, −3). All lie on the vertical line x = −2, which is parallel to the y-axis.

Medium
A square has one vertex at (1, 1) and its centre at the origin. Find the other three vertices.
Show Answer

By symmetry about the origin: (1,1), (−1,1), (−1,−1), (1,−1). The other three vertices are (−1, 1), (−1, −1), (1, −1).

Hard
A point lies in Quadrant II at equal distances from both axes. If that distance is 5 units, write its coordinates.
Show Answer

Quadrant II: x < 0, y > 0. Equal distances: |x| = |y| = 5. Coordinates: (−5, 5).

Medium
Ananya marks four points: (2, 3), (−2, 3), (−2, −3), (2, −3). She joins them in order. What is the perimeter of the shape formed? (Use the grid values.)
Show Answer

The shape is a rectangle. Width = 2 − (−2) = 4; Height = 3 − (−3) = 6. Perimeter = 2(4 + 6) = 20 units.

A = (4, −2) → reflect across y-axis: A' = (−4, −2) → reflect across x-axis: A'' = (−4, 2).

If a point and its reflection across the x-axis both lie on the x-axis, what can you say about the original point?

If the reflection across the x-axis is on the x-axis, then y and −y are both 0, so y = 0. The point already lies on the x-axis.

Since a > b > 0, both b and a are positive, so (b, a) is in Quadrant I. It is just a different point in the same quadrant.

The point (−3, −4) is reflected across the x-axis. What are the new coordinates?

(−3, 4) — in Quadrant II.

A shape has vertices at (2,1), (5,1), (5,4), (2,4). After reflection across the x-axis, write the new vertices.

If Q = (2, 3) and R = (−2, 3), are they reflections of each other across any axis? Which one?

Aarav plots A(−1,−1), B(1,−1), C(1,1), D(−1,1) and joins them. What is the area of figure ABCD?

Plot the points (0,5), (5,0), (0,−5), (−5,0). What shape do they form? Calculate its area.

The four points form a square (a rhombus with equal diagonals) rotated 45°. Diagonals = 10 each. Area = ½ × d₁ × d₂ = ½ × 10 × 10 = 50 square units.

Hard
Give coordinates of 4 points, one in each quadrant, each exactly 5 units from the origin (do NOT use the axes).

Examples (using 3-4-5 triangle): Q I: (3,4); Q II: (−3,4); Q III: (−3,−4); Q IV: (3,−4). Each satisfies 3²+4²=25, so distance = 5.

Hard
The point P(x, y) is plotted such that x = y. For what special line does P always lie on?
Show Answer

P lies on the line y = x, which passes through the origin at 45° to both axes (in Q I and Q III).

Medium
A town planner places a park at (−4, 2) and a school at (4, 2). Are they the same distance from the y-axis? From the x-axis?
Show Answer

Distance from y-axis = |x| → |−4| = 4 and |4| = 4. Yes, equidistant from y-axis. Distance from x-axis = |y| → both are 2. Yes, equidistant from x-axis too.

Medium
Seema plots two points that are mirror images across the y-axis. The first point is (7, −3). What are the possible coordinates of the second point?
Show Answer

Mirror across y-axis → negate x. Second point: (−7, −3).

A graph of temperature vs. time shows a point at (3, 25). What does this mean in context?

At time = 3 units (e.g., 3 hours), the temperature was 25°.

Easy
A distance-time graph has a point at (0, 5). What does the y-coordinate 5 mean?

Medium

Both points have the same y-coordinate (y = 3), so the segment is horizontal, parallel to the x-axis, with length 4 − 1 = 3 units.

Easy

Medium
A graph shows a quadrilateral with vertices at (1,1), (4,1), (4,4), (1,4). What shape is this and what are its dimensions?

Easy

Show Answer

Base = distance from (0,0) to (6,0) = 6. Height = perpendicular distance from (3,4) to the x-axis = 4. Area = ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 sq units.

Medium
From a graph you read the points (−2, 4) and (−2, −4). Are they symmetric about the x-axis or the y-axis?

Same x-coordinate, opposite y-coordinates → symmetric about the x-axis.

Easy
