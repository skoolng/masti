SET A Foundation - Basic Concepts

Name three states of matter and one property of each.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Solid: fixed shape; Liquid: fixed volume; Gas: compressible.

Define physical change and chemical change.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Physical change does not form new substance; chemical change does.

Classify: melting wax, burning wax.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Melting is physical; burning is chemical.

What is diffusion? Give one daily-life example.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Movement of particles from high to low concentration; perfume spreading in room.

State the law of conservation of mass.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Mass is neither created nor destroyed in a closed system.

Differentiate element, compound, and mixture.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Element: one atom type; compound: chemically combined elements; mixture: physical combination.

Name one method to separate sand and water.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Filtration.

Name one method to separate salt from saltwater.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Evaporation or distillation.

What is solute and solvent in sugar water?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Solute: sugar; Solvent: water.

Why does heating usually increase dissolving speed?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Higher temperature increases particle motion and collisions.

State one evidence of chemical reaction in vinegar + baking soda.

If concentration doubles, what can happen to reaction rate generally?
Hard

Difference between saturated and unsaturated solution?
Medium

Give one safety rule for handling acids in school lab.
