SET A Foundation - Basic Concepts

List the seven life processes (MRS GREN).
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, Nutrition.

State the function of cell membrane and nucleus.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Membrane controls entry/exit; nucleus controls cell activities.

Name one difference between plant and animal cells.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Plant cells have cell wall and chloroplasts; animal cells do not.

Define photosynthesis in one sentence.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Plants use light, CO2, and water to produce glucose and oxygen.

Write word equation for photosynthesis.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Carbon dioxide + water -> glucose + oxygen (light, chlorophyll).

Define balanced diet.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Diet containing appropriate proportions of nutrients, water, and fiber.

Name three major nutrient groups and one function each.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Carbohydrates-energy; proteins-growth/repair; fats-energy storage.

What is adaptation? Give one example.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Feature helping survival, e.g., cactus spines reduce water loss.

Why are enzymes important in digestion?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: They speed up breakdown of large food molecules.

Distinguish between inherited and acquired traits.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Inherited come from genes; acquired develop due to environment/experience.

SET B Core Practice - Skill Building

A meal has 60 g carbs, 20 g protein, 15 g fat. Which is the main energy source?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Carbohydrates are the main immediate energy source.

Why does deficiency of iron affect health?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: It can reduce hemoglobin causing fatigue/anemia.

Give one function of xylem and phloem.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Xylem transports water/minerals; phloem transports sugars.

How does exercise affect heart rate and breathing rate?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Both increase to meet oxygen demand.

State one reason biodiversity is important.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: It improves ecosystem stability and resilience.

What is habitat and niche?
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Habitat is where an organism lives; niche is its role in ecosystem.

Construct a simple food chain with producer, consumer, decomposer.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Grass -> rabbit -> fox; decomposers act on remains.

What is transpiration?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Loss of water vapor from plant leaves via stomata.

How do vaccines help immunity?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: They train immune system to recognize pathogens safely.

Differentiate aerobic and anaerobic respiration.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Aerobic uses oxygen and releases more energy; anaerobic uses no oxygen and less energy.

SET C Application - Context and Reasoning

Plan an experiment to test effect of light on photosynthesis using pondweed.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Measure oxygen bubble rate at different light intensities while controlling variables.

Name two control variables in pondweed experiment.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Water temperature and CO2 concentration.

Data: bubbles/min at 10,20,30 cm from lamp are 40,26,15. Interpret trend.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Bubble rate decreases as distance increases (light intensity decreases).

Explain why temperature control is needed in this setup.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Temperature also affects enzyme activity and photosynthesis rate.

Suggest one improvement for more accurate oxygen measurement.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Use gas syringe instead of counting bubbles.

Design a 3-day food log template to evaluate balanced diet.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Include meal, nutrients, water, fruits/vegetables, notes.

How could you estimate pulse recovery after exercise?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Measure pulse immediately and at regular intervals until baseline returns.

What does faster recovery usually indicate?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Better cardiovascular fitness.

State one ethical issue in human body experiments.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Privacy/consent when collecting health data.

Write CER on whether increased light increased photosynthesis in your data.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Claim with evidence from bubble rates and reasoning via light-dependent reactions.

Explain one human activity that threatens biodiversity and one mitigation.

Answer: Habitat loss from construction; mitigation: protected green zones/restoration.

Describe energy transfer inefficiency in food chains.
Hard

Answer: Most energy is lost as heat/metabolism between trophic levels.

Why are apex predators important in ecosystems?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: They regulate populations and maintain trophic balance.

Medium
Show Answer

Compare short-term vs long-term health effects of poor diet.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Short-term fatigue; long-term obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular risk.

How can data literacy improve personal health decisions?
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Interpreting nutrition and activity data guides better choices.

Write one evidence-based recommendation for your age group nutrition.
Medium
Show Answer
