Easy

An invisible channel of ionised air that extends from cloud to ground in steps before the main lightning stroke

What is the return stroke of lightning?
Easy
Answer

The main visible lightning flash — a massive surge of current from ground to cloud along the ionised path

Return stroke: ~30,000 A, lasts ~30 microseconds

Easy

The negative bottom of the cloud repels electrons in the ground → electrons move away → ground surface becomes positively charged (induction)

Induction over large distances — same principle as electrostatics
What causes thunder?
Easy
Answer

Rapid heating of air around the lightning channel (to 30,000 K) causes explosive expansion → pressure wave → thunder

Thunder = sound of rapid air expansion from lightning's heat
Why does lightning travel to the ground from clouds?
Easy
Answer

Air between cloud base and ground breaks down when the electric field exceeds ~3 million V/m → ionised path forms → discharge occurs

Air becomes conducting when electric field is strong enough

Medium
Answer

A closed conducting shell (metal car body, metal cage) → electric field inside is zero (charges distribute on outside surface). Lightning current flows around outside → no field inside → occupants protected. Car is safer than open ground during storms.

Contraindicated: elderly/sick patients in ICUs who cannot be moved safely.

Evaluate the role of insurance and financial instruments in cyclone risk management for developing countries.
Hard
Answer

Traditional insurance: under-penetrated in developing countries (high premium, claim disputes, complex contracts).
