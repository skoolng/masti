Show that n and n+1 share no common factor greater than 1 (consecutive integers are coprime).
Hard
Answer

Answer: Proven. GCD(n, n+1) = 1 always.

Method: Proof by Contradiction
Step 1: Suppose d > 1 divides both n and n+1.
Step 2: Then d divides (n+1) − n = 1. But no integer d > 1 divides 1.
Step 3: Contradiction → GCD(n, n+1) = 1 (they are coprime).
Key Rule: Consecutive integers always have GCD = 1 (coprime).

Step 3: 5 and 7 both divide n → 35 divides n.
Key Rule: If gcd(a,b)=1 and a|n and b|n, then ab|n.

What is the largest factor of n that is less than n itself? What is this called?
Medium
Answer
