Is 7 a factor of 56? Explain why.
Easy
Answer

Answer: Yes, 7 is a factor of 56

Method: Division check
Step 1: Divide 56 ÷ 7 = 8
Step 2: Check remainder: 7 × 8 = 56, remainder = 0
Step 3: Since remainder = 0, 7 divides 56 exactly, so 7 is a factor
Key Rule: A number a is a factor of b if b ÷ a leaves remainder 0
Is 9 a factor of 50? Explain why.
Easy
Answer

Answer: No, 9 is not a factor of 50

Method: Division check
Step 1: Divide 50 ÷ 9 = 5 remainder 5
Step 2: Check: 9 × 5 = 45, and 50 − 45 = 5 (remainder is not zero)
Step 3: Since remainder ≠ 0, 9 does not divide 50 exactly
Key Rule: A number is NOT a factor if the division leaves a non-zero remainder

Easy
Answer

Answer: True

Prove or disprove: if a is a factor of b and b is a factor of c, then a is a factor of c. Give an example.
Hard
Answer

Answer: True — this is the transitive property of divisibility

Method: Algebraic proof
Step 1: If a|b, then b = a×k for some integer k
Step 2: If b|c, then c = b×m = (a×k)×m = a×(km) for some integer m
Step 3: Since km is an integer, a|c ✓. Example: 3|12 and 12|60, so 3|60 (60÷3=20) ✓
Key Rule: Divisibility is transitive: if a divides b and b divides c, then a divides c
