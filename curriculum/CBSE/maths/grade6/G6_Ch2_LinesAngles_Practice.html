Point, Line, Line Segment, Ray

Set A Basic Identification

A pencil tip resting on paper represents a _____ in geometry.
Easy
Answer

Answer: Point

Method: Recall and Apply Definition
Step 1: A point has no length, width, or thickness — just a position in space.
Step 2: A pencil tip resting still marks exactly one location with no area.
Step 3: This matches the definition of a point perfectly.
Key Rule: A point represents an exact location in space with no dimensions.
How many endpoints does a line segment have?
Easy
Answer

Answer: Two endpoints

Method: Definition Recall
Step 1: A line segment is a part of a line that is bounded at both ends.
Step 2: It has a definite start and a definite finish — these are its endpoints.
Step 3: Count: one endpoint at each end = 2 endpoints total.
Key Rule: A line segment has exactly two endpoints and a measurable length.
A torch beam shining from a flashlight into the darkness models which geometric figure — line, ray, or segment?
Easy
Answer

Answer: Ray

Method: Match Real Object to Geometric Figure
Step 1: A line has no endpoints; a segment has two; a ray has exactly one endpoint.
Step 2: The flashlight is a fixed starting point (one endpoint).
Step 3: The beam shoots out in one direction into the dark — no end point, it just goes on.
Key Rule: A ray has one endpoint and extends infinitely in one direction only.

Easy
Answer

Answer: Line

Method: Apply Definition of a Line
Step 1: A line has no endpoints and extends forever in both directions.
Step 2: The road stretches endlessly — no start, no end, both ways.
Step 3: This matches a line exactly (not a segment or ray).
Key Rule: A line extends infinitely in both directions and has no endpoints.

Step 1: A point has no length; a segment has two ends; a line has no ends.

On a number line, the segment from –3 to +5 has how many units of length?
Easy
Answer

Answer: 8 units

Method: Distance on a Number Line
Step 1: Identify the two endpoints: –3 and +5.
Step 2: Length = larger value – smaller value = 5 – (–3).
Step 3: 5 – (–3) = 5 + 3 = 8 units.

Set B Applying Properties & Word Problems

Points A and B are marked on a straight road. How many line segments can be drawn between them?
Easy
Answer

Answer: One line segment

Method: Apply the Unique Segment Postulate
Step 1: Two points A and B are given on a straight road.
Step 2: Between any two points, there is exactly one straight line path.
Step 3: Therefore, exactly one segment AB can be drawn connecting them.
Key Rule: Through any two points, there is exactly one straight line (and one segment).
A builder places three fence posts P, Q, and R in a straight line. Between how many pairs of posts can he measure a segment?
Easy
Answer

Answer: 3 segments

Method: List All Pairs of Points
Step 1: The three posts are P, Q, and R (collinear in order).
Step 2: List all pairs: PQ, QR, and PR.
Step 3: Count the pairs: PQ (P to Q), QR (Q to R), PR (P to R) = 3 segments total.
Key Rule: The number of segments from n collinear points is C(n,2) = n(n–1)/2.

Medium
Answer

Answer: 6 segments

Step 1: No three points are collinear, so every pair of points forms a unique segment.
Step 2: Number of ways to choose 2 points from 4 = C(4,2) = 4 × 3 ÷ 2.
Step 3: C(4,2) = 12 ÷ 2 = 6 distinct segments.
Key Rule: With n non-collinear points, the number of segments = C(n,2) = n(n–1)/2.
A ray OA starts at O and passes through A. Does the ray also pass through a point B that lies beyond A on the same straight path? Explain.
Medium
Answer

Answer: Yes, the ray also passes through B

Method: Understanding a Ray's Infinite Reach
Step 1: Ray OA starts at O and passes through A, then continues infinitely in that direction.
Step 2: Point B is beyond A on the exact same straight path from O.
Step 3: Since the ray has no end, it covers all points in that direction, including B.

In a coordinate plane, point P is at (2, 3) and point Q is at (6, 3). Find the length of segment PQ.
Medium
Answer

Answer: 4 units

Method: Horizontal Distance in a Coordinate Plane
Step 1: P = (2, 3) and Q = (6, 3) — both have the same y-coordinate (y = 3).
Step 2: Since y values are equal, PQ is a horizontal segment. Length = |x2 – x1|.
Step 3: PQ = |6 – 2| = 4 units.
Key Rule: For a horizontal segment, length = |difference in x-coordinates|.
A student says "Two rays OA and OB are the same if they have the same starting point O." Is this correct? Why or why not?
Medium
Answer

Answer: No, this is incorrect

Method: Compare Rays by Direction
Step 1: Two rays from the same starting point can go in many different directions.
Step 2: Ray OA goes toward A; ray OB goes toward B — if A and B are in different directions, the rays are different.
Step 3: Two rays are the same only if they share both the same endpoint AND the same direction.
Key Rule: Rays are the same if and only if they have the same endpoint and same direction.
A school's straight corridor runs from the main gate (point M) to the library (point L). A student walks from M to L. Is this path a line, ray, or segment? What if the student continued walking past L?
Medium
Answer

Answer: Line segment ML; then a ray starting at M

Method: Identify Endpoints Before and After
Step 1: M to L is a fixed start and fixed end — two endpoints = a line segment ML.
Step 2: If the student continues past L, the path now starts at M and has no end on the L side.
Step 3: One endpoint (M) + goes on past L = a ray starting at M through L.
Key Rule: Adding an endpoint makes a ray; two endpoints make a segment; no endpoints make a line.
Mark points A, B, C on a line in that order. State whether each is true or false: (a) AB + BC = AC (b) AC – BC = AB
Medium
Answer

Answer: (a) True, (b) True

Method: Segment Addition Postulate
Step 1: If B is between A and C on a line, then A, B, C are collinear with B in the middle.
Step 2: (a) AB + BC = AC — this is the segment addition postulate: the two smaller parts add up to the whole. TRUE.
Step 3: (b) AC – BC = AB — this is just (a) rearranged by subtracting BC from both sides. TRUE.
Key Rule: If B is between A and C, then AB + BC = AC (Segment Addition Postulate).

A town planner marks 5 bus stops A, B, C, D, E along a straight road in that order. AB = 2 km, BC = 1.5 km, CD = 2.5 km, DE = 1 km. What is the total length AE?
Medium
Answer

Answer: 7 km

Method: Segment Addition Along a Straight Road
Step 1: List all consecutive segments: AB = 2, BC = 1.5, CD = 2.5, DE = 1 (all in km).
Step 2: Add them: 2 + 1.5 = 3.5, then 3.5 + 2.5 = 6, then 6 + 1 = 7.
Step 3: AE = 7 km. Check: all stops are on a straight road so addition is valid.
Key Rule: If points are collinear in order, the total length equals the sum of all consecutive segments.
Point M is the midpoint of segment XY. XM = 4.5 cm. What is XY?
Medium
Answer

Answer: 9 cm

Method: Midpoint Property
Step 1: M is the midpoint of XY, which means M divides XY into two equal halves.
Step 2: XM = MY = 4.5 cm (both halves are equal).
Step 3: XY = XM + MY = 4.5 + 4.5 = 9 cm.
Key Rule: The midpoint divides a segment into two equal parts, so the full length = 2 × half.
On a straight athletics track, the start line is point S and the finish is point F. A runner runs from S to F (100 m), turns around, and runs back 40 m to point P. Express SP and PF as line segments and find their lengths.
Medium
Answer

Answer: SP = 60 m, PF = 40 m

Method: Segment Subtraction
Step 1: The full track is SF = 100 m. The runner ran to F then came back 40 m to point P.
Step 2: P is 40 m from F, so segment PF = 40 m.
Step 3: SP = SF – PF = 100 – 40 = 60 m. So SP = 60 m and PF = 40 m.
Key Rule: If B is between A and C, then AB = AC – BC.
Three collinear points A, B, C satisfy AC = 15 cm and BC = 6 cm. Find AB if B is between A and C.
Medium
Answer

Answer: AB = 9 cm

Method: Segment Addition / Subtraction
Step 1: B is between A and C, so AB + BC = AC (segment addition postulate).
Step 2: Substitute: AB + 6 = 15.
Step 3: AB = 15 – 6 = 9 cm.
Key Rule: If B is between A and C, then AB = AC – BC.
A surveyor marks points P, Q, R, S on a straight boundary fence. PQ = QR = RS = 8 m. A gate post is placed at the midpoint of PS. How far is the gate from P?
Medium

Step 2: The midpoint of PS is at PS ÷ 2 = 24 ÷ 2 = 12 m from P.
Step 3: The gate is placed 12 m from P (which falls on segment QR, 4 m past Q).

Two rays OA and OB are drawn from the same point O in exactly opposite directions. Together, what do OA and OB form?

How many line segments are formed by 6 collinear points? How does this compare to 6 non-collinear points (no three collinear)?
Hard
Answer

Answer: Both give 15 segments, but collinear ones overlap

Method: Apply C(n,2) and Compare Arrangements
Step 1: Formula: C(6,2) = 6 × 5 ÷ 2 = 15 for any 6 points.

A straight pipe runs from valve V to tank T. A repair joint J divides it so that VJ : JT = 3 : 5. If VT = 40 m, find VJ and JT.
Hard
Answer

Answer: VJ = 15 m, JT = 25 m

Method: Divide a Segment in a Given Ratio
Step 1: VJ : JT = 3 : 5, so total parts = 3 + 5 = 8 parts.
Step 2: Each part = 40 ÷ 8 = 5 m.
Step 3: VJ = 3 × 5 = 15 m; JT = 5 × 5 = 25 m. Check: 15 + 25 = 40 m ✓

On a number line, ray R1 starts at –2 and goes right; ray R2 starts at 4 and goes left. Do these rays overlap? If so, describe the overlapping region.
Hard
Answer

Answer: Overlap is the segment [–2, 4], length 6 units

Method: Identify Each Ray's Coverage and Find Intersection
Step 1: Ray R1 starts at –2 and goes right: it covers all numbers ≥ –2, i.e. [–2, +∞).
Step 2: Ray R2 starts at 4 and goes left: it covers all numbers ≤ 4, i.e. (–∞, 4].
Step 3: Overlap = numbers that are both ≥ –2 AND ≤ 4 = [–2, 4], length = 4 – (–2) = 6 units.

In a coordinate plane, segment AB has A=(–3, 1) and B=(5, 1). Point C divides AB such that AC : CB = 1 : 3. Find the coordinates of C.
Hard
Answer

Answer: C = (–1, 1)

Method: Section Formula (Internal Division)
Step 1: AC : CB = 1 : 3, so C is 1/(1+3) = 1/4 of the way from A to B.
Step 2: x-coordinate of C = x_A + (1/4)(x_B – x_A) = –3 + (1/4)(5 – (–3)) = –3 + (1/4)(8) = –3 + 2 = –1.
Step 3: y-coordinate = 1 (same for all, since A and B both have y = 1). So C = (–1, 1).
Key Rule: To find a point dividing a segment in ratio m:n, move m/(m+n) of the total distance from the first point.

Answer
