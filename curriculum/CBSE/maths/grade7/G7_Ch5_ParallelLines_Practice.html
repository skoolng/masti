Basic Geometry Terms (Point, Line, Ray, Line Segment)

SET A Foundational Recall

What is a point in geometry? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: A point is an exact location in space. It has no length, width, or thickness. It is represented by a dot and named with a capital letter (e.g., point A).

Key Rule: A point has zero dimensions — no size, only position.

What is a line? How does it differ from a line segment? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: A line extends infinitely in both directions with no endpoints. A line segment has two definite endpoints and a fixed length.

Key Rule: Line → endless both ways. Line segment → has two endpoints, finite length.

Define a ray and write its symbolic notation if it starts at point P and passes through point Q. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: A ray starts at a fixed point (the endpoint) and extends infinitely in one direction. Notation: →PQ (ray PQ, starting at P through Q).

Key Rule: Ray has one endpoint and goes on forever in one direction.

Name the geometric figure: It has two endpoints A and B and a definite measurable length. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Line segment AB (written as AB̄).

Key Rule: Two endpoints + fixed length = line segment.

How many lines can pass through a single given point? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Infinitely many lines can pass through a single point.

Key Rule: Through one point → infinite lines possible.

How many lines can pass through two given distinct points? Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Exactly one line can pass through two distinct points.

Key Rule: Two distinct points determine a unique line.

True or False: A ray PQ and ray QP are the same ray. Explain. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: False. Ray PQ starts at P and goes toward Q and beyond. Ray QP starts at Q and goes toward P and beyond. They have different starting points and go in opposite directions.

Key Rule: The starting endpoint and direction determine a ray — order matters.

A line is named AB. Can the same line also be called BA? Why? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Yes. A line has no fixed direction or endpoints, so line AB and line BA refer to the same line.

Key Rule: For lines, AB = BA. For rays, AB ≠ BA.

Three points X, Y, Z lie on the same line with Y between X and Z. Name two rays and one line segment you can identify. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Rays: ray XY (or XZ) and ray ZY (or ZX). Line segment: YZ (or XY, or XZ).

Key Rule: Collinear points allow segments between any two, and rays from any endpoint through the others.

What is the difference between collinear and non-collinear points? Give an example of each. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Collinear points lie on the same line (e.g., A, B, C on line l). Non-collinear points do not all lie on one line (e.g., vertices of a triangle A, B, C).

Key Rule: Collinear = same line. Non-collinear = not all on same line; they form a triangle.

SET B Identification and Notation

Write the correct notation for a line passing through points M and N. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Line MN (written with a double-headed arrow above: ↔MN).

Key Rule: A line is denoted with a double-headed arrow above the two letters.

Identify: "Starts at point A, goes through B and continues forever." What figure is this? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Ray AB. It starts at A (endpoint) and extends infinitely through B.

Key Rule: One endpoint + infinite extension in one direction = ray.

A line segment PQ has length 8 cm. If R is the midpoint of PQ, what are the lengths of PR and RQ? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: PR = RQ = 4 cm (each half of 8 cm).

Key Rule: Midpoint divides a segment into two equal halves.

How many line segments can be drawn using 3 non-collinear points? Name them. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: 3 line segments: AB, BC, and CA.

Key Rule: Number of segments from n points = n(n−1)/2 = 3(2)/2 = 3.

Points A, B, C, D are on a line in that order. List all possible line segments. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: AB, BC, CD, AC, BD, AD — a total of 6 line segments.

Key Rule: 4 collinear points → C(4,2) = 6 segments.

True or False: Two distinct lines can intersect at more than one point. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: False. Two distinct lines can intersect at most at one point.

Key Rule: Two lines intersect at exactly one point (or are parallel and never meet).

What do we call lines that lie in the same plane but never intersect? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Parallel lines.

Key Rule: Parallel lines are coplanar lines that never meet, maintaining equal distance apart.

Name the figure formed when two rays share the same endpoint. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: An angle. The shared endpoint is called the vertex.

Key Rule: Two rays from a common endpoint form an angle.

Segment AB = 15 cm. Point C is on AB such that AC = 9 cm. Find CB. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: CB = AB − AC = 15 − 9 = 6 cm.

Step 1: CB = AB − AC = 15 − 9 = 6 cm.
Key Rule: Whole segment = sum of its parts.

Can a ray have a length? Explain. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: No. A ray extends infinitely in one direction, so it has no definite length. Only a line segment has a finite, measurable length.

Key Rule: Only line segments have finite length. Lines and rays are infinite.

Describe the difference between a line and a line segment using the idea of endpoints. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: A line has no endpoints — it continues forever in both directions. A line segment has exactly two endpoints and a finite length between them.

Key Rule: Endpoints = 0 → line; Endpoints = 2 → line segment; Endpoints = 1 → ray.

Can two rays form a complete line? Under what condition? Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Yes — two rays form a line if they have the same endpoint and point in exactly opposite directions (forming a straight angle of 180°).

Key Rule: Opposite rays from the same endpoint form a straight line.

Explain why a "line" in geometry is different from a "line" drawn on paper. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: A geometric line is perfectly straight, has zero width, and extends infinitely in both directions. A drawn line has a finite length, some width (pen thickness), and slight imperfections.

Key Rule: Geometry deals with ideal, abstract figures; drawings are physical approximations.

Point O is the midpoint of both segments AC and BD. What can you say about AB and CD? Hard
Show Answer

Answer: AO = OC and BO = OD. So diagonals AC and BD bisect each other at O — this is the property of a parallelogram's diagonals.

Key Rule: If the same point bisects two different segments, those segments bisect each other.

Name all line segments in a figure with 4 points where no 3 are collinear. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: C(4,2) = 6 segments: AB, AC, AD, BC, BD, CD.

Key Rule: 4 non-collinear points → 6 segments (forms a complete quadrilateral).

Two lines intersect at point O. How many rays are formed at O? Name the type of angles formed. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: 4 rays are formed at O. The angles formed are two pairs of vertically opposite angles.

Key Rule: Two intersecting lines → 4 rays → 4 angles → 2 pairs of vertical angles.

Can three points be both collinear and form a triangle? Explain. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: No. Three collinear points lie on the same line and cannot form a triangle. A triangle requires three non-collinear points.

Key Rule: Triangle → 3 non-collinear points. Collinear → all on same line, no enclosed area.

XY = 5a + 3 and YZ = 3a + 9. If Y is the midpoint of XZ, find a and XZ. Hard

Answer: a = 3, XZ = 36 cm.

Step 1: XY = YZ → 5a+3 = 3a+9 → 2a = 6 → a = 3.
Step 2: XY = 5(3)+3 = 18 cm. XZ = 2×18 = 36 cm.
Key Rule: Midpoint → two halves equal.

Angles — Types and Measurement

SET A Angle Types — Recall

Define an acute angle and give its range. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: An acute angle is an angle whose measure is greater than 0° and less than 90°. Range: 0° < θ < 90°.

Key Rule: Acute → less than 90°.

What is a right angle? Give two real-life examples. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: A right angle measures exactly 90°. Examples: corner of a square, corner of a book/table.

Key Rule: Right angle = exactly 90°, shown with a small square symbol.

Classify the angle 135°. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: 135° is an obtuse angle (between 90° and 180°).

Key Rule: Obtuse → greater than 90° and less than 180°.

What is a straight angle? Draw and describe it. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: A straight angle measures exactly 180°. The two rays form a straight line going in opposite directions from the vertex.

Key Rule: Straight angle = 180° = a flat line.

Classify the angle 270°. Easy
Show Answer

Answer: 270° is a reflex angle (between 180° and 360°).

Key Rule: Reflex → greater than 180° and less than 360°.

Which type of angle measures exactly 360°? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: A complete angle (or full angle) measures 360°. It represents one full rotation.

Key Rule: Full rotation = 360° = complete angle.

An angle measures 47°. What type is it? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Acute angle (47° is between 0° and 90°).

Key Rule: 0° < 47° < 90° → acute.

An angle measures 90°. What is its type and what symbol is used to mark it? Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Right angle. It is marked with a small square (□) at the vertex.

Key Rule: Right angle symbol = small square at the corner.

List all five types of angles in increasing order of measure. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Zero angle (0°), Acute (0°–90°), Right (90°), Obtuse (90°–180°), Straight (180°), Reflex (180°–360°), Complete (360°).

Key Rule: Zero → Acute → Right → Obtuse → Straight → Reflex → Complete.

An angle and its reflex angle together make 360°. If the angle is 110°, find its reflex angle. Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Reflex angle = 360° − 110° = 250°.

Step 1: Reflex = 360° − given angle = 360° − 110° = 250°.
Key Rule: An angle + its reflex = 360°.

Define complementary angles. Do they have to be adjacent? Easy
Show Answer

Key Rule: Complementary → sum = 90°. Adjacency is not required.
