What is an equation? Give an example.
Easy
Answer

An equation is a mathematical statement that two expressions are equal (LHS = RHS)

Step 1: An equation has two sides: Left Hand Side (LHS) and Right Hand Side (RHS).
Step 2: They are connected by '=' sign.
Step 3: Example: x + 5 = 12.
Step 4: The value of x that makes this true is the solution.
Key Rule: Equation: LHS = RHS; a mathematical statement of equality.
Which of these are equations? (a) 3+4 (b) 2x=10 (c) x+5>7 (d) 7=7
Easy
Answer

(b) 2x=10 and (d) 7=7 are equations

Step 1: Equation: has '=' sign.
Step 2: (a) 3+4 is an expression (no equals).
Step 3: (b) 2x=10 ✓ equation.
Step 4: (c) x+5>7 is an inequality (not equation). (d) 7=7 ✓ equation.
Key Rule: Equation must have '=' sign; expression has no equals sign.
What is the difference between an expression and an equation?
Easy
Answer

Expression: no equals sign (e.g., 3x+5). Equation: has equals sign (e.g., 3x+5=20).

Step 1: Expression: a combination of numbers and variables without '='.
Step 2: Equation: two expressions connected by '='.
Step 3: Example: 3x+2 is expression; 3x+2=14 is equation.
Key Rule: Expression: no '='. Equation: two expressions with '=' between them.

Step 1: Left of '=' sign: 2x+3 = LHS.
Step 2: Right of '=' sign: 11 = RHS.
Key Rule: LHS: left side of '='; RHS: right side of '='.

Medium
Answer

Explain the balance principle in equations.
Easy
Answer

Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other side

Step 1: An equation is like a balance scale: both sides must stay equal.
Step 2: Add same number to both sides: still balanced.
Step 3: Subtract same: balanced. Multiply: balanced. Divide: balanced.
Key Rule: Balance principle: same operation on both sides preserves equality.

Easy

Step 2: RHS: 12+3=15.
Step 3: New equation: 8+x=15.
Step 4: The solution (x=7) is unchanged.
Key Rule: Adding same to both sides: equation equivalent but not simplified (for solving, subtract).
