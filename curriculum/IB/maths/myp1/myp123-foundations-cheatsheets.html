Numbers, Place Value, and Properties

Number fluency is the base for almost everything else. These are the patterns and properties students should know automatically by MYP 1 and still use confidently in MYP 2 and MYP 3.

Place Value

Reading and Building Numbers

  • The value of a digit depends on its place: ones, tens, hundreds, thousands, and beyond.
  • In decimals, places move right as tenths, hundredths, thousandths.
  • Expanded form helps show value: `5,482 = 5,000 + 400 + 80 + 2`.
  • When comparing numbers, compare the greatest place value first.
10 ones = 1 ten 10 tenths = 1 one 0.25 = 25 hundredths
Operations

Commutative, Associative, and Identity Rules

  • Commutative: order can change for addition and multiplication. `a + b = b + a`, `ab = ba`.
  • Associative: grouping can change for addition and multiplication. `(a + b) + c = a + (b + c)`.
  • Identity: adding `0` or multiplying by `1` keeps the number the same.
  • Subtraction and division are not commutative or associative.
7 + 4 = 4 + 7 (2 × 3) × 5 = 2 × (3 × 5) n + 0 = n

Fractions, Decimals, Percentages, Ratio, and Integers

Fractions

Fraction Basics

  • Equivalent fractions come from multiplying or dividing numerator and denominator by the same non-zero number.
  • To add or subtract fractions, use a common denominator first.
  • To multiply fractions, multiply numerators and denominators.
  • To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.
a/b + c/b = (a + c)/b a/b × c/d = ac/bd a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c
Decimals & Percent

Switching Forms

  • Percent means “out of 100”.
  • Convert percent to decimal by dividing by 100.
  • Convert decimal to percent by multiplying by 100.
  • Increase or decrease by a percent using multiplier form.
35% = 0.35 0.47 = 47% New value = original × (1 ± rate)
Ratio & Integers

Signs, Ratios, and Rates

  • Same signs multiply or divide to give a positive result.
  • Different signs multiply or divide to give a negative result.
  • Ratios compare quantities; rates compare quantities with different units.
  • Unit rate means “for one”.
(+)(+) = + (-)(-) = + (-)(+) = - Unit rate = amount / 1 unit

Algebra Rules and Equation Skills

Expressions

Order of Operations and Like Terms

  • Use order of operations: brackets, powers, multiply/divide, add/subtract.
  • Like terms must have the same variable part and same powers.
  • `3x + 5x = 8x`, but `3x + 5y` cannot be combined.
  • Substitute carefully using brackets when numbers replace variables.
2x + 4x = 6x 3(a + 2) 5² = 25
Properties

Distributive Property and Expansion

  • The distributive property spreads multiplication across addition or subtraction.
  • `a(b + c) = ab + ac`
  • `a(b - c) = ab - ac`
  • Factorising is the reverse of distribution.
4(x + 3) = 4x + 12 6y + 12 = 6(y + 2)
Equations

Balancing Equations

  • Do the same operation to both sides to keep an equation balanced.
  • Undo addition/subtraction before multiplication/division in simple equations.
  • Check by substituting the answer back into the original equation.
  • For linear graphs, remember `y = mx + c`: slope and intercept matter.
x + 5 = 12 → x = 7 3x = 21 → x = 7 y = mx + c

Geometry, Measurement, and Transformations

Angles & Shapes

Essential Geometry Facts

  • Angles on a straight line add to `180°`.
  • Angles around a point add to `360°`.
  • Vertically opposite angles are equal.
  • Interior angles of a triangle add to `180°`.
  • Interior angles of a quadrilateral add to `360°`.
Triangle sum = 180° Around a point = 360°
Measurement

Perimeter, Area, and Volume

  • Perimeter is the total distance around a shape.
  • Area measures the surface inside a 2D shape.
  • Volume measures the space inside a 3D object.
Rectangle area = l × w Triangle area = 1/2 × b × h Prism volume = area of cross-section × length
Coordinate & Transform

Coordinates, Symmetry, and Moves

  • Coordinates are written `(x, y)`.
  • Translation slides a shape without turning it.
  • Reflection flips a shape in a mirror line.
  • Rotation turns a shape around a fixed point.
  • Congruent shapes are same size and shape; similar shapes keep shape but not size.
(x, y) Translation = slide Reflection = flip Rotation = turn

Data, Statistics, and Probability

Statistics

Reading and Summarising Data

  • Mean: add the values and divide by how many there are.
  • Median: middle value when data is in order.
  • Mode: value that appears most often.
  • Range: largest value minus smallest value.
Mean = sum / number of values Range = max - min
Probability

Chance and Simple Events

  • Probability is between `0` and `1`.
  • `0` means impossible, `1` means certain.
  • Probability = favourable outcomes / total outcomes.
  • Use fractions, decimals, or percentages to describe probability.
P(event) = favourable / total 0 ≤ P(event) ≤ 1
Graphs

Graph Habits

  • Always read the title, axis labels, and units first.
  • Scale marks must increase evenly.
  • Choose the graph type that matches the data: bar, line, pie, scatter, or stem-and-leaf.
  • Trend means the overall pattern in the data.

What Students Should Know Before Practice

This quick checklist helps students see whether they are ready for chapter work across MYP 1, MYP 2, and MYP 3.

MYP 1 Ready

Students should be confident with place value, operations, fractions, percentages, perimeter, area, and simple algebraic expressions.

  • Compare and order numbers
  • Use divisibility rules
  • Apply distributive property
  • Read simple tables and charts
MYP 2 Ready

Students should build on MYP 1 by adding integers, ratio, rates, equations, geometry, and probability.

  • Work with negative numbers
  • Solve one-step and two-step equations
  • Interpret rates and ratio
  • Calculate basic probability
MYP 3 Ready

Students should combine number skill, algebra skill, geometry, and data reasoning more fluently.

  • Plot coordinates and read graphs
  • Recognise linear patterns
  • Work with transformations
  • Compare bivariate data and trends
