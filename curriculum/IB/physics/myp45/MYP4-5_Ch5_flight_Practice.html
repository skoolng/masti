SET A Foundation - Core Concepts

Define lift in the context of Flight.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: lift means the upward force that opposes weight.

Define drag and state one unit if applicable.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: drag is the resistive force opposing motion through air.. Force unit: newton (N).

List two everyday examples related to Flight.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Airplane wings and paper gliders are valid examples.

State one key difference between lift and drag.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Lift acts mainly upward; drag acts opposite to motion.

Write one labelled diagram you would draw for Flight.
Medium

Answer: A force diagram with lift, weight, thrust, and drag arrows is acceptable.

Explain one common misconception in Flight and correct it.
Medium
