Tangent to a Circle — Definition and Properties

SET A Definition & Basic

Define a tangent to a circle. How many points does a tangent have in common with the circle?Easy
Show Answer

A tangent is a line that touches the circle at exactly one point (the point of tangency). It has exactly one point in common with the circle.

How does a tangent differ from a secant? Draw both for the same circle.Easy
At point P on a circle of centre O and radius 5 cm, a tangent is drawn. What is the angle between the tangent and the radius OP?Easy
Show Answer

90° (tangent is perpendicular to radius at point of contact)

Can a tangent be drawn to a circle from a point inside the circle? Explain why or why not.Easy
Show Answer

No. From an interior point, any line through it crosses the circle at two points (a secant), never just touching it.

State two properties of a tangent to a circle.Easy
Show Answer

(1) A tangent touches the circle at exactly one point. (2) The tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.

From an external point P, two tangents are drawn to a circle of radius 5 cm. If the tangent length is 12 cm, find the distance OP.Medium
Show Answer

OP = √(5²+12²) = √169 = 13 cm

The tangent at point A on circle O is horizontal. What is the direction of OA?Easy
Show Answer

OA is vertical (perpendicular to the horizontal tangent)

A wheel rolls along a flat road. At any instant, the road is tangent to the wheel. What is the relationship between the contact point, the road, and the wheel's centre?Easy
Show Answer

The radius from the wheel's centre to the contact point is perpendicular to the road (tangent), so the centre is directly above the contact point.

In a circle, the tangent at point T makes an angle of 50° with chord TQ. Name the angle in the alternate segment that equals 50°.Medium
True or False: A tangent can be parallel to a diameter. Justify with a diagram.Medium
Show Answer

True. A tangent at either endpoint of the diameter is perpendicular to the diameter, so two such tangents are parallel to each other but they're not parallel to the diameter. Tangent perpendicular to the diameter... A tangent at a point where the diameter is perpendicular (i.e. at the endpoints of a perpendicular diameter) IS parallel to the original diameter. Yes, this is possible.
