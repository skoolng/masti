The Mole Concept

SET A Recall & Understand (Easy)

What is a mole in chemistry?
Show Answer

A mole is the SI unit for amount of substance. It is defined as exactly 6.022 × 10²³ elementary entities (atoms, molecules, ions, etc.). This number is Avogadro's number (Nₐ).

Easy
State the value of Avogadro's number.
Show Answer

Nₐ = 6.022 × 10²³ mol¹. One mole of any substance contains this many particles.

Easy
Why was Avogadro's number chosen as the basis for the mole?
Show Answer

It is the number of C-12 atoms in exactly 12 g of carbon-12, linking atomic mass units (u) directly to grams. This makes 1 mole of C-12 weigh exactly 12 g.

Easy
How many atoms are in 1 mole of iron (Fe)?
Show Answer

1 mole of Fe contains exactly 6.022 × 10²³ iron atoms.

Easy
How many molecules are in 1 mole of water (H₂O)?
Show Answer

6.022 × 10²³ molecules of H₂O.

Easy
What does the mole concept allow chemists to do practically?
Show Answer

It bridges the microscopic world (individual atoms) with the macroscopic world (measurable grams). Chemists count particles by weighing them using a balance.

Easy
If you have 2 moles of CO₂, how many molecules is that?
Show Answer

2 × 6.022 × 10²³ = 1.204 × 10²⁴ molecules of CO₂.

Easy
State the formula connecting number of particles (N), moles (n), and Avogadro's number (Nₐ).
Show Answer

N = n × Nₐ. Rearranged: n = N / Nₐ.

Easy
Can the mole refer to things other than atoms? Give an example.
Show Answer

Yes. The mole applies to any entity: molecules, ions, electrons, formula units. Example: 1 mole of NaCl contains 6.022 × 10²³ Na¹ ions AND 6.022 × 10²³ Cl¹ ions.

Easy
Explain the analogy: "a mole is to chemists as a dozen is to bakers."
Show Answer

A dozen = 12 items (a convenient counting unit). A mole = 6.022 × 10²³ items. Both are fixed numbers simplifying counting of large quantities. The difference is scale: atoms are far too small to count individually, so the mole is much larger than a dozen.

Easy

SET B Apply & Analyse (Medium)

Calculate the number of atoms in 3 moles of aluminium.
Show Answer

N = 3 × 6.022 × 10²³ = 1.807 × 10²⁴ atoms of Al.

Medium
How many moles is 3.011 × 10²³ molecules of O₂?
Show Answer

n = 3.011 × 10²³ / 6.022 × 10²³ = 0.5 mol of O₂.

Medium
1 mole of H₂O contains how many hydrogen atoms?
Show Answer

Each H₂O has 2 H atoms. So 1 mol H₂O → 6.022 × 10²³ × 2 = 1.204 × 10²⁴ hydrogen atoms.

Medium
A student says "1 mole of hydrogen" — why is this ambiguous?
Show Answer

Hydrogen exists as H atoms (in compounds) or H₂ molecules (elemental). 1 mol H atoms = 6.022 × 10²³ atoms. 1 mol H₂ = 6.022 × 10²³ molecules = 1.204 × 10²⁴ atoms. Should specify H or H₂.

Medium
How many oxygen atoms are in 0.5 mol of CO₂?
Show Answer

0.5 mol CO₂ = 3.011 × 10²³ molecules. Each has 2 O atoms → 6.022 × 10²³ O atoms.

Medium
Calculate the number of ions in 0.25 mol of NaCl.
Show Answer

NaCl gives 2 ions per formula unit. Total = 0.25 × 6.022 × 10²³ × 2 = 3.011 × 10²³ ions.

Medium
If 1.204 × 10²⁴ atoms of a metal are present, how many moles is this?
Show Answer

n = 1.204 × 10²⁴ / 6.022 × 10²³ = 2 mol.

Medium
A balloon contains 4.516 × 10²³ molecules of helium. Convert to moles and estimate volume at STP.
Show Answer

n = 4.516 × 10²³ / 6.022 × 10²³ = 0.75 mol. Volume at STP = 0.75 × 22.4 = 16.8 L.

Medium
How many total atoms are in 1 mol of glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)?
Show Answer

Atoms per molecule: 6+12+6 = 24. Total = 6.022 × 10²³ × 24 = 1.445 × 10²⁵ atoms.

Medium
How does the mole concept solve the practical problem of counting atoms in a lab?
Show Answer

Atoms cannot be counted directly. By defining the mole as 6.022 × 10²³ particles, chemists can weigh a substance and calculate n = mass/molar mass, then N = n × Nₐ. A balance reading gives an exact particle count.

Medium

SET C Investigate & Calculate (Hard — MYP+DP Bridge)

If Nₐ were 1 × 10²⁴ instead of 6.022 × 10²³, how would this change the mass of one atom of carbon-12?
Show Answer

Currently: mass per atom = 12 / (6.022 × 10²³) = 1.993 × 10²³ g. If Nₐ = 10²⁴: mass per atom = 12 / 10²⁴ = 1.2 × 10²³ g. The atom would appear lighter by a factor of 10/6.022 ≈ 1.66, breaking the link between atomic mass units and g/mol.

Hard
Estimate how long (in years) it would take to count to Avogadro's number at 1 count per second.
Show Answer

Seconds/year ≈ 3.156 × 10&sup7;. Years = 6.022 × 10²³ / 3.156 × 10&sup7; ≈ 1.9 × 10¹⁶ years — about 1.4 million times the age of the universe.

Hard
A sample contains 9.033 × 10²³ atoms of sulfur. (a) moles? (b) mass? (c) total electrons? (S: Z=16, M=32)
Show Answer

(a) n = 9.033×10²³/6.022×10²³ = 1.5 mol. (b) m = 1.5×32 = 48 g. (c) Each S has 16 electrons → 9.033×10²³×16 = 1.445×10²⁵ electrons.

Hard
Explain why C-12 (not H) is the reference for Avogadro's number and atomic mass.
Show Answer

C-12 was chosen in 1961 because: (1) carbon is a stable solid, easy to handle precisely; (2) C-12 is a pure, well-defined isotope; (3) most chemical work involves organic carbon compounds; (4) nearly integer atomic masses for most elements are preserved. Hydrogen gas is difficult to handle with sufficient precision.

Hard
Compare 1 mol each of H₂, O₂, N₂: same molecules, same atoms, same mass?
Show Answer

Same molecules: yes (each = 6.022×10²³). Same atom count: yes (all diatomic → 1.204×10²⁴ atoms each). Same mass: NO. H₂=2 g, N₂=28 g, O₂=32 g. Equal moles = equal particle count but very different masses.

Hard
The density of water is 1 g/mL. How many molecules in one drop (0.05 mL)?
Show Answer

m = 0.05 g. n = 0.05/18 = 2.78×10³ mol. N = 2.78×10³×6.022×10²³ = 1.67×10²¹ molecules in one drop.

Hard
In the 2019 SI redefinition, Nₐ was fixed exactly. What does "fixed" mean and why does it matter?
Show Answer

"Fixed" means the value has zero uncertainty by definition (6.02214076×10²³ mol¹ exactly). Previously it was experimentally measured with uncertainty. Fixing it grounds the mole on a fundamental constant, ensuring global consistency in chemical measurements.

Hard
Calculate the mass of a single water molecule in grams.
Show Answer

mass = M / Nₐ = 18 / 6.022×10²³ = 2.99×10²³ g per molecule (≈3×10²³ g).

Hard
Compare 1 mol of Ne atoms vs 1 mol of Ne gas at STP: are they the same?
Show Answer

Yes — neon is monatomic, so "1 mol Ne atoms" and "1 mol Ne gas" are identical. Particles: 6.022×10²³ Ne atoms. Mass: 20.18 g. Volume at STP: 22.4 L. No ambiguity unlike diatomics (O vs O₂).

Hard
A swimming pool holds 2×10⁶ L of water. Estimate the number of water molecules.
Show Answer

m = 2×10⁶×1000 g = 2×10⁹ g. n = 2×10⁹/18 = 1.11×10⁸ mol. N = 1.11×10⁸×6.022×10²³ ≈ 6.7×10³¹ molecules.

SET A Recall & Understand (Easy)

Define molar mass and state its units.
Show Answer

Molar mass (M) is the mass of one mole of a substance. Units: g/mol. Numerically equal to the relative atomic or formula mass.

Easy
Calculate the molar mass of H₂O. (H=1, O=16)
Show Answer

M = 2(1) + 16 = 18 g/mol.

Easy
Calculate the molar mass of NaCl. (Na=23, Cl=35.5)
Show Answer

M = 23 + 35.5 = 58.5 g/mol.

Easy
Calculate the molar mass of CO₂. (C=12, O=16)
Show Answer

M = 12 + 2(16) = 44 g/mol.

Easy
Calculate the molar mass of CaCO₃. (Ca=40, C=12, O=16)
Show Answer

M = 40 + 12 + 3(16) = 100 g/mol.

Easy
Calculate the molar mass of H₂SO₄. (H=1, S=32, O=16)
Show Answer

M = 2(1) + 32 + 4(16) = 98 g/mol.

Easy
What is the molar mass of O₂?
Show Answer

M(O₂) = 2 × 16 = 32 g/mol.

Easy
How is relative atomic mass (Ar) related to molar mass?
Show Answer

Numerically equal but different concepts: Ar is dimensionless (a ratio); molar mass M = Ar g/mol. E.g., Ar(Fe)=56 → M(Fe)=56 g/mol.

Easy
Calculate M for Ca(OH)₂. (Ca=40, O=16, H=1)
Show Answer

M = 40 + 2(16+1) = 40 + 34 = 74 g/mol.

Easy
What does it mean to say "the molar mass of CO₂ is 44 g/mol"?
Show Answer

One mole (6.022 × 10²³ molecules) of CO₂ has a total mass of 44 g. Each molecule has a relative mass of 44 u.

Easy

Calculate M of MgCl₂ and % Mg. (Mg=24, Cl=35.5)

Calculate M of ethanol C₂H₅OH. (C=12, H=1, O=16)

Medium
Explain why M(Cl₂) = 71 g/mol, not 35.5 g/mol.

Chlorine exists as diatomic Cl₂ molecules. Each molecule has 2 Cl atoms: M = 2 × 35.5 = 71 g/mol.

Medium
A student says "1 g of H₂ and 1 g of O₂ contain the same number of molecules." Correct or not? Use molar masses.
Show Answer

Incorrect. n(H₂)=1/2=0.5 mol; n(O₂)=1/32=0.031 mol. H₂ contains 16× more molecules. Equal mass ≠ equal moles.

Medium
Calculate % N in ammonium nitrate NH₄NO₃. (N=14, H=1, O=16)
Show Answer

M = 18 + 62 = 80 g/mol. Mass N = 2×14 = 28. % N = 28/80 × 100 = 35%.

SET C Investigate & Calculate (Hard — MYP+DP Bridge)

A compound is 40% C, 6.67% H, 53.33% O. Find its empirical formula. (C=12, H=1, O=16)
Show Answer

Per 100 g: C=40/12=3.33 mol; H=6.67/1=6.67 mol; O=53.33/16=3.33 mol. Ratio 1:2:1 → empirical formula CH₂O.

Hard
Empirical formula CH₂O; molar mass 180 g/mol. Find the molecular formula.
Show Answer

M(CH₂O)=30. n=180/30=6. Molecular formula = C₆H₁₂O₆ (glucose).

Hard
Explain why two compounds can have the same molar mass but different formulas. Give an example.
Show Answer

Molar mass depends only on total atomic masses, not arrangement. CO and N₂ both M=28 g/mol but completely different substances. C₂H₆O (ethanol) and CH₂O₂ (formic acid) both M=46 g/mol.

Hard
A metal oxide has 70% iron. What is its formula? (Fe=56, O=16)
Show Answer

Per 100 g: Fe=70/56=1.25 mol; O=30/16=1.875 mol. Ratio Fe:O = 1.25:1.875 = 2:3 → Fe₂O₃.

Hard
Calculate M of haemoglobin if 3.32×10³ g contains 3.32×10⁹ mol.
Show Answer

M = 3.32×10³ / 3.32×10⁹ = 1×10⁶ g/mol. (Illustrative values showing how M is found from n and m for large biomolecules.)

Hard
Chlorine: Cl-35 (75%) and Cl-37 (25%). Show how Ar = 35.5 is calculated.
Show Answer

Ar = (35×0.75) + (37×0.25) = 26.25 + 9.25 = 35.5. This weighted average reflects natural isotope abundances.

Hard
A drug (M=300 g/mol) is 60% carbon. How many C atoms are in one molecule?
Show Answer

Mass C per mol = 0.60×300 = 180 g. Carbon atoms = 180/12 = 15 C atoms per molecule.

Hard
Hydrate Na₂CO₃·xH₂O has M=286 g/mol. Find x. (Na=23, C=12, O=16, H=1)
Show Answer

M(Na₂CO₃)=106. Water mass = 286-106=180. x = 180/18 = 10. Compound is washing soda Na₂CO₃·10H₂O.

Hard
Calculate M of caffeine C₈H₁₀N₄O₂. (C=12, H=1, N=14, O=16)
Show Answer

M = 8(12)+10(1)+4(14)+2(16) = 96+10+56+32 = 194 g/mol.

Hard
Critically compare "molar mass" vs "relative molecular mass." Are they the same?
Show Answer

Relative molecular mass (Mr) is dimensionless — a ratio with no units. Molar mass M has units g/mol. Numerically equal (Mr H₂O=18; M=18 g/mol). Mr is used for comparing masses on relative scale; M is used in all practical n=m/M calculations.

SET A Recall & Understand (Easy)

What is stoichiometry?
Show Answer

Stoichiometry is the quantitative study of reactants and products in chemical reactions. It uses balanced equations and mole ratios to calculate amounts of substances reacting or produced.

Easy
In 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O, what is the mole ratio of H₂ to O₂?
Show Answer

2:1. Two moles of H₂ react with one mole of O₂. The coefficients in a balanced equation give the mole ratios.

Easy
Why must a chemical equation be balanced before doing stoichiometry?
Show Answer

A balanced equation obeys conservation of mass (atoms are neither created nor destroyed). The coefficients give the correct mole ratios needed for calculations.

Easy
In N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃, how many moles of NH₃ form from 1 mol N₂?
Show Answer

2 moles of NH₃ (ratio N₂:NH₃ = 1:2).

Easy
What is a limiting reagent?
Show Answer

The limiting reagent is the reactant that is completely consumed first and determines (limits) how much product can form. The other reactant is in excess.

Easy
In 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O, if 4 g of H₂ reacts with 32 g of O₂, what mass of H₂O forms? (M: H₂=2, O₂=32, H₂O=18)
Show Answer

n(H₂)=4/2=2 mol; n(O₂)=32/32=1 mol. Ratio 2:1 → exact stoichiometric amounts. n(H₂O)=2 mol → m=2×18=36 g. (Also: conservation of mass: 4+32=36 g.)

Easy
Define excess reagent.
Show Answer

The excess reagent is the reactant that remains after the limiting reagent is fully consumed. It is present in more than the stoichiometric amount required.

Easy
Balance: Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃. State the mole ratio Fe:O₂:Fe₂O₃.
Show Answer

4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃. Ratio = 4:3:2.

Easy
In N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃, how many moles of H₂ are needed to react with 2 mol N₂?
Show Answer

Ratio N₂:H₂ = 1:3. For 2 mol N₂ → 2×3 = 6 mol H₂.

Easy
What is conservation of mass and how does it apply to stoichiometry?
Show Answer

Mass is neither created nor destroyed. In stoichiometry: total mass of reactants = total mass of products. Balanced equations ensure atom counts balance, satisfying this law.

Easy

SET A Recall & Understand (Easy)

Define theoretical yield.
Show Answer

Theoretical yield is the maximum amount of product calculated from stoichiometry, assuming the reaction goes to completion with no losses.

Easy
Define actual yield.
Show Answer

Actual yield is the amount of product actually obtained in the laboratory from an experiment.

Easy
State the formula for percentage yield.
Show Answer

% yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100%.

Easy
Can percentage yield ever exceed 100%? Explain.
Show Answer

No, in principle. If actual > theoretical, it means the product contains impurities (e.g., unreacted starting material, solvent) or measurement error. A true % yield is always 0–100%.

Easy
Give three reasons why actual yield is less than theoretical yield.
Show Answer

(1) Reaction does not go to completion (equilibrium). (2) Side reactions producing unwanted products. (3) Product lost during purification (filtering, transferring, evaporation).

Easy
A reaction has theoretical yield 50 g and actual yield 35 g. Calculate % yield.
Show Answer

% yield = 35/50 × 100 = 70%.

Easy
Why is a high percentage yield important in industrial chemistry?
Show Answer

Higher yield means less raw material wasted, lower costs, and reduced environmental impact. In pharmaceutical production, even a 1% improvement in yield can save millions of dollars.

Easy
What does a 100% yield mean?
Show Answer

Every mole of limiting reagent has been converted to product with no losses. This is the theoretical maximum and is rarely achieved in practice.

Easy
Is percentage yield a measure of purity? Explain.
Show Answer

No. Percentage yield measures how much product was obtained compared to theoretical. Purity measures how free the product is from impurities. A sample can have 90% yield but still contain impurities.

Easy
If % yield = 80% and theoretical yield = 25 g, what is the actual yield?
Show Answer

Actual = (80/100) × 25 = 20 g.

At 450°C and 200 atm the equilibrium position favours only ~15% conversion, because the reaction is exothermic and the temperature needed for reasonable rate shifts equilibrium left. The unreacted N₂ and H₂ are recycled to improve overall efficiency.

Medium
Distinguish between % yield and atom economy. Give an example where both matter.
Show Answer

% yield = actual/theoretical × 100% (measures how much product was collected). Atom economy = (M of desired product / M of all products) × 100% (measures how efficiently atoms end up in the product). A reaction can have 100% yield but low atom economy (e.g., many by-products). Both matter in green chemistry.

Why might a reaction run at lower temperature give higher yield even though it is slower?

For exothermic reversible reactions, lower temperature shifts equilibrium toward products (Le Chatelier) → higher equilibrium yield. The trade-off is a slower rate. Industry balances rate and yield by choosing an optimum temperature.

SET A Recall & Understand (Easy)

State the definition of pH.
Show Answer

pH = −log₁₀[H¹], where [H¹] is the concentration of hydrogen ions in mol/L. pH is a logarithmic scale measuring acidity/alkalinity.

Easy
What pH does a neutral solution have at 25°C?
Show Answer

pH = 7 at 25°C. At this pH, [H¹] = [OH¹] = 1×10&sup7; mol/L.

Easy
If pH = 3, what is [H¹]?
Show Answer

[H¹] = 10³ = 0.001 mol/L = 1×10³ mol/L.

Easy
If [H¹] = 0.01 mol/L, calculate pH.
Show Answer

pH = −log(0.01) = −log(10²) = −(−2) = 2.

Easy
Which is more acidic: pH 2 or pH 4? By how many times?
Show Answer

pH 2 is more acidic. Each pH unit represents a 10× difference in [H¹]. pH 2 vs pH 4 = 2 units → 10² = 100 times more acidic.

Easy
What is the difference between a strong acid and a weak acid in terms of dissociation?
Show Answer

Strong acid (e.g., HCl): fully dissociates in water → [H¹] = initial concentration. Weak acid (e.g., CH₃COOH): partially dissociates → [H¹] < initial concentration.

Easy
Calculate the pH of a 0.1 mol/L HCl solution (assume full dissociation).
Show Answer

[H¹] = 0.1 mol/L. pH = −log(0.1) = −(−1) = 1.

Easy
What is pOH and how is it related to pH at 25°C?
Show Answer

pOH = −log[OH¹]. At 25°C: pH + pOH = 14. So pOH = 14 − pH.

Easy
If pH = 11, is the solution acidic, neutral, or basic? What is [H¹]?
Show Answer

pH 11 > 7 → basic (alkaline). [H¹] = 10₋¹¹ = 1×10₋¹¹ mol/L.

Easy
Why is the pH scale logarithmic rather than linear?
Show Answer

Because [H¹] spans an enormous range (10&sup0; to 10₋¹⁴). A logarithmic scale compresses this to a convenient 0–14 range, where each unit is a 10-fold change.

pH 5 vs pH 7: difference = 2 units → 10² = 100 times more H¹ than pure water.
