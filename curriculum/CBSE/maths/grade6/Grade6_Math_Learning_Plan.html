Patterns in Mathematics

🗓 Learning Plan (2–3 weeks)

Week 1 – See & Describe
  • Spot patterns in tiles, fabric, nature
  • Colour patterns (AB, ABB, ABC)
  • Shape sequences
  • Number sequences: even, odd, multiples
Week 2 – Rules & Formulas
  • Arithmetic sequences (common difference)
  • Triangular & square numbers
  • Fibonacci sequence
  • Patterns in multiplication tables
Week 3 – Create & Extend
  • Build your own sequence and share the rule
  • Dot/matchstick patterns
  • Finding the nth term
  • Mini-project: pattern in real life
Tip: Keep a Pattern Journal — draw or describe one new pattern every day for a week.

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

What comes next?   2, 4, 6, 8, ___
Easy
Answer

10 (add 2 each time)

Continue the pattern: A, B, A, B, A, ___
Easy
Answer

B (repeating AB pattern)

Find the missing number: 5, 10, 15, ___, 25
Easy
Answer

20 (multiples of 5)

What are the next two triangular numbers?   1, 3, 6, 10, ___, ___
Easy
Answer

15 and 21 (add 5, then 6)

Identify the rule and find the next term: 3, 6, 12, 24, ___
Medium
Answer

48 (each term is multiplied by 2)

What are the next two numbers in the Fibonacci sequence?   1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, ___, ___
Medium
Answer

13 and 21 (add the previous two numbers)

Matchsticks are arranged as triangles in a row (△△△…). Triangle 1 needs 3 sticks, Triangle 2 needs 5 sticks total, Triangle 3 needs 7 sticks total. How many sticks for the 8th triangle?
Medium
Answer

Pattern: 2n + 1 → 2(8) + 1 = 17 sticks

The square numbers are 1, 4, 9, 16, 25 … What is the 12th square number? What is the difference between the 12th and 11th square numbers?
Medium
Answer

12² = 144; 11² = 121; difference = 23 (always an odd number)

A staircase pattern: Step 1 uses 1 block, Step 2 uses 3 blocks, Step 3 uses 6 blocks. These are triangular numbers. How many blocks are needed for Step 7? For Steps 1–7 in total?
Hard
Answer

Step 7 = 28 blocks; Total of Steps 1–7 = 1+3+6+10+15+21+28 = 84 blocks

A farmer plants trees in an orchard in rows: Row 1 has 1 tree, Row 2 has 3, Row 3 has 5, Row 4 has 7 … (a) Write a rule for Row n. (b) How many trees in Row 15? (c) What is the total number of trees in 10 rows?
Hard
Answer

(a) Row n = 2n − 1   (b) 2(15)−1 = 29 trees   (c) Sum of first 10 odd numbers = 10² = 100 trees

🗓 Learning Plan (2–3 weeks)

  • Measuring angles with a protractor

Week 3 – Pairs & Problems

  • Adjacent angles

Tip: Use a clock to practise angles — the hands make different angles at different times!

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

Name each: a point, a line, a ray, and a line segment. How are they different?
Easy
Answer

A point has no dimension; a line goes forever in both directions; a ray starts at a point and goes forever in one direction; a line segment has two endpoints.

Classify each angle: (a) 45°   (b) 90°   (c) 135°   (d) 180°
Easy
Answer

(a) Acute   (b) Right   (c) Obtuse   (d) Straight

What type of angle does a clock show at 3:00? At 6:00?
Easy
Answer

3:00 → Right angle (90°); 6:00 → Straight angle (180°)

Two angles are complementary. One angle is 38°. Find the other.
Easy
Answer

90° − 38° = 52°

Two angles are supplementary. One angle is 73°. Find the other.
Medium
Answer

180° − 73° = 107°

Two lines intersect. One pair of vertically opposite angles is 65°. Find all four angles formed.
Medium
Answer

Vertically opposite angles are equal. The other pair = 180° − 65° = 115°. Angles: 65°, 115°, 65°, 115°

A triangle has angles 55° and 75°. Find the third angle. What type of triangle is it?
Medium
Answer

180° − 55° − 75° = 50°. All angles are acute → Acute triangle

Angle AOB and angle BOC are adjacent angles on a straight line (i.e., AOC is a straight line). If ∠AOB = 3x° and ∠BOC = 2x°, find x and both angles.
Medium
Answer

3x + 2x = 180° → 5x = 180° → x = 36°. ∠AOB = 108°, ∠BOC = 72°

Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. The transversal makes an angle of 110° with the first line (obtuse side). List all eight angles formed and their measures.
Hard
Answer

Alternate interior angles are equal; corresponding angles are equal. Angles: 110°, 70°, 110°, 70° (at first line) and 110°, 70°, 110°, 70° (at second line — same pattern due to parallel lines)

In a triangle PQR, the exterior angle at Q is 130°. The angle at P is 55°. Find the angle at R. (Use the exterior angle theorem.)
Hard
Answer

Exterior angle = sum of two non-adjacent interior angles: ∠P + ∠R = 130° → 55° + ∠R = 130° → ∠R = 75°

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

Write the number 3,07,456 in words.
Easy
Answer

Three lakh seven thousand four hundred and fifty-six

Arrange in ascending order: 4321, 4213, 4132, 4312
Easy
Answer

4132 < 4213 < 4312 < 4321

Round 6,748 to (a) the nearest 10, (b) the nearest 100, (c) the nearest 1000.
Easy
Answer

(a) 6,750   (b) 6,700   (c) 7,000

Is 432 divisible by 3? Show using the divisibility rule.
Easy
Answer

4 + 3 + 2 = 9; 9 is divisible by 3. So yes, 432 is divisible by 3.

Check whether 756 is divisible by 2, 3, 4, 6, and 9.
Medium
Answer

÷2: yes (ends in 6) · ÷3: yes (7+5+6=18) · ÷4: yes (56÷4=14) · ÷6: yes (both 2&3) · ÷9: yes (18÷9=2)

Using the digits 3, 5, 7 (each exactly once), form (a) the greatest 3-digit number, (b) the smallest 3-digit number.
Medium
Answer

(a) 753   (b) 357

A 3 × 3 magic square uses numbers 1–9, each once, so every row, column, and diagonal sums to 15. Fill in the missing numbers: the centre is 5, top-right is 2, bottom-left is 4.
Medium
Answer

Standard 3×3 magic square:
2 7 6
9 5 1
4 3 8

I am a 4-digit number. I am divisible by 5 and 9. My digits add to 18. I am between 3000 and 4000. What am I?
Medium
Answer

3645 (3+6+4+5=18, ends in 5 so ÷5, digit sum 18 so ÷9, between 3000–4000)

A palindrome number reads the same forwards and backwards (e.g., 121). How many 3-digit palindromes are there? List them systematically.
Hard
Answer

Form: ABA where A ∈ {1…9} (9 choices) and B ∈ {0…9} (10 choices). Total = 9 × 10 = 90 palindromes

Karim has ₹10,000. He buys 3 books at ₹485 each, 2 pens at ₹38 each, and a bag for ₹1,295. (a) Estimate the total cost by rounding to the nearest hundred. (b) Find the exact change he gets.
Hard
Answer

(a) 3×500 + 2×0 + 1300 = ≈ ₹2,800 (approx)   (b) Exact: 3×485 + 2×38 + 1295 = 1455+76+1295 = 2826; Change = 10000−2826 = ₹7,174

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

Show the number 17 using tally marks.
Easy
Answer

|||| |||| |||| || (three groups of 5 + 2)

A bar graph shows favourite colours: Red=12, Blue=18, Green=7, Yellow=10. Which colour is most popular? Least popular?
Easy
Answer

Most popular: Blue (18); Least popular: Green (7)

Find the mean of: 8, 12, 15, 9, 11.
Easy
Answer

(8+12+15+9+11) ÷ 5 = 55 ÷ 5 = 11

Find the median of: 3, 7, 1, 9, 5.
Easy
Answer

Arrange: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9. Middle value = 5

Scores: 4, 7, 4, 9, 4, 8, 6. Find the mode.
Medium
Answer

4 (appears 3 times)

Marks of 6 students: 72, 85, 90, 60, 72, 83. Find the mean, median, and mode.
Medium
Answer

Mean = (72+85+90+60+72+83)÷6 = 462÷6 = 77
Sorted: 60,72,72,83,85,90 → Median = (72+83)÷2 = 77.5
Mode = 72

A pictograph uses ☀ = 5 students. Class A has 4 suns, Class B has 6 suns, Class C has 3 suns. How many students in total? Which class has the most?
Medium
Answer

A=20, B=30, C=15. Total = 65 students. Class B has the most.

A double bar graph compares scores of Boys and Girls in 3 subjects. Boys: Math 75, Science 80, English 65. Girls: Math 85, Science 70, English 90. In which subject do girls outperform boys by the most?
Medium
Answer

Math: 85−75=10; Science: 70−80=−10; English: 90−65=25. Girls outperform boys most in English (by 25 marks)

The mean of 5 numbers is 20. Four of the numbers are 15, 22, 18, and 25. Find the fifth number.
Hard
Answer

Sum = 20 × 5 = 100. Known sum = 15+22+18+25 = 80. Fifth number = 100 − 80 = 20

Weekly temperatures (°C): Mon 32, Tue 35, Wed 28, Thu 30, Fri 33, Sat 36, Sun 31. (a) Find mean, median, mode. (b) On how many days was the temperature above the mean?
Hard
Answer

Sum = 225; Mean = 225÷7 ≈ 32.1°C
Sorted: 28,30,31,32,33,35,36 → Median = 32°C; Mode = none (all unique)
Above 32.1°C: Tue(35), Fri(33), Sat(36) → 3 days

Prime Time

🗓 Learning Plan (3 weeks)

  • Multiples of a number
  • Factor pairs
  • Common factors & common multiples
Week 2 – Primes & Composites
  • Prime numbers (only 2 factors)
  • Composite numbers
  • Sieve of Eratosthenes
  • Prime factorisation (factor tree)
Week 3 – HCF & LCM
  • HCF by listing & prime factorisation
  • LCM by listing & prime factorisation
  • Relationship: HCF × LCM = product
  • Word problems
Tip: Build a Sieve of Eratosthenes on grid paper — circle all primes up to 100 by crossing out multiples!

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

List all factors of 24.
Easy
Answer

1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24

Is 37 prime or composite? How do you know?
Easy
Answer

Prime — its only factors are 1 and 37.

Write the first 5 multiples of 7.
Easy
Answer

7, 14, 21, 28, 35

Find the HCF of 18 and 24.
Easy
Answer

Factors of 18: 1,2,3,6,9,18 · Factors of 24: 1,2,3,4,6,8,12,24 · HCF = 6

Find the LCM of 6 and 8 using prime factorisation.
Medium
Answer

6 = 2×3; 8 = 2³. LCM = 2³×3 = 24

Express 180 as a product of prime factors (draw a factor tree).
Medium
Answer

180 = 2² × 3² × 5

Find the HCF and LCM of 36 and 48. Verify: HCF × LCM = 36 × 48.
Medium
Answer

36=2²×3²; 48=2⁴×3. HCF=2²×3=12; LCM=2⁴×3²=144. Check: 12×144=1728; 36×48=1728 ✓

Two bells ring at intervals of 12 minutes and 18 minutes. They ring together at 9:00 AM. When will they next ring together?
Medium
Answer

LCM(12,18) = 36 minutes. Next together at 9:36 AM

Find the smallest number divisible by 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6.
Hard
Answer

LCM(2,3,4,5,6): 4=2²; 3; 5; 6=2×3. LCM = 2²×3×5 = 60

A school has 60 boys and 48 girls. They are to be divided into teams of equal size (same number in each team, with no one left out, teams can be mixed). What is the largest possible team size? How many teams will there be?
Hard
Answer

HCF(60,48) = 12 (largest team size). Total students = 108. Teams = 108÷12 = 9 teams

Perimeter and Area

🗓 Learning Plan (2–3 weeks)

Week 1 – Perimeter
  • Concept: distance around a shape
  • Perimeter of rectangles & squares
  • Perimeter of triangles & polygons
  • Real-life: fencing, borders
Week 2 – Area
  • Concept: space inside a shape
  • Area by counting squares
  • Area formulas: rectangle, square
  • Area of triangles
Week 3 – Applications
  • Same perimeter, different areas
  • Same area, different perimeters
  • Tiling/flooring problems
  • Composite shapes
Tip: Measure your room with a ruler — calculate its perimeter and area. Compare with a classmate's room!

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

Find the perimeter of a rectangle with length 8 cm and width 5 cm.
Easy
Answer

P = 2(l+b) = 2(8+5) = 2×13 = 26 cm

Find the area of a square with side 6 cm.
Easy
Answer

A = s² = 6² = 36 cm²

A garden has perimeter 36 m. If its length is 10 m, what is its width?
Easy
Answer

2(10+b) = 36 → 10+b = 18 → b = 8 m

Find the area of a triangle with base 12 cm and height 7 cm.
Easy
Answer

A = ½ × base × height = ½ × 12 × 7 = 42 cm²

A room is 9 m long and 6 m wide. How many square tiles of side 30 cm are needed to cover the floor?
Medium
Answer

Room area = 9×6 = 54 m². Tile area = 0.3×0.3 = 0.09 m². Tiles = 54÷0.09 = 600 tiles

Two rectangles: Rectangle A is 12 cm × 4 cm; Rectangle B is 8 cm × 6 cm. Compare their perimeters and areas.
Medium
Answer

Perimeter A = 32 cm; Perimeter B = 28 cm. Area A = 48 cm²; Area B = 48 cm². Same area, different perimeters!

A wire of length 40 cm is bent into a rectangle. Find the dimensions that give the maximum area.
Medium
Answer

Perimeter = 40 cm → l+b = 20. Maximum area when l=b (square): 10×10 = 100 cm²

An L-shaped room: main rectangle 10m×8m, with a 3m×4m rectangular cut from one corner. Find the perimeter and area of the L-shape.
Medium
Answer

Area = 10×8 − 3×4 = 80−12 = 68 m². Perimeter = 10+8+3+4+7+4 = 36 m (trace all edges)

A farmer has 120 m of fencing to enclose a rectangular field. One side is along a river (no fence needed). What dimensions give the largest area?
Hard
Answer

Three sides: l + 2b = 120. Area = lb = (120−2b)b = 120b−2b². Maximum when b = 30 m → l = 60 m. Max area = 1800 m²

A rectangular park (50m × 40m) has a path of 2m width running inside along all four sides. Find the area of the path alone.
Hard
Answer

Outer area = 50×40 = 2000 m². Inner rectangle = (50−4)×(40−4) = 46×36 = 1656 m². Path area = 2000−1656 = 344 m²

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

A pizza is cut into 8 equal slices. If you eat 3 slices, what fraction did you eat?
Easy
Answer

3/8

Convert 11/4 to a mixed number.
Easy
Answer

11 ÷ 4 = 2 remainder 3 → 2¾

Are 2/3 and 8/12 equivalent? Show how.
Easy
Answer

2/3 × 4/4 = 8/12 ✓ Yes, they are equivalent.

Which is greater: 3/5 or 5/8?
Easy
Answer

LCM of 5,8 = 40. 3/5=24/40; 5/8=25/40. 5/8 is greater.

Add: 2/7 + 3/7 + 5/7
Medium
Answer

(2+3+5)/7 = 10/7 = 1 3/7

Subtract: 5/6 − 3/8
Medium
Answer

LCM(6,8)=24. 20/24 − 9/24 = 11/24

Multiply: 3/4 × 8/9. Simplify your answer.
Medium
Answer

(3×8)/(4×9) = 24/36 = 2/3

Riya walked 2¾ km in the morning and 1½ km in the evening. How far did she walk in total? How much more did she walk in the morning?
Medium
Answer

Total: 2¾ + 1½ = 2¾ + 1 2/4 = 4¼ = 4.25 km. Difference: 2¾ − 1½ = 1¼ = 1.25 km more in morning

A recipe needs 2⅓ cups of flour. If you make 1½ times the recipe, how many cups of flour do you need?
Hard
Answer

2⅓ × 3/2 = 7/3 × 3/2 = 7/2 = 3½ cups

A tank is ¾ full. After adding 30 litres it is 9/10 full. What is the total capacity of the tank?
Hard
Answer

9/10 − 3/4 = 18/20 − 15/20 = 3/20 of tank = 30 L. Total capacity = 30 ÷ (3/20) = 30 × 20/3 = 200 litres

Tip: Always keep your pencil sharp for accurate constructions. Label every point you draw!

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

Draw a line segment PQ of length 6.5 cm using a ruler.
Easy
Answer

Mark point P, measure 6.5 cm on ruler, mark Q. Draw line connecting P and Q. ✓

Using a compass, draw a circle with radius 3 cm. Label the centre O and mark a point on the circumference as A.
Easy
Answer

Place compass at O with width 3 cm and draw the complete circle. ✓

Draw a line segment AB = 8 cm. Construct its perpendicular bisector using a compass.
Easy
Answer

With compass > 4 cm, draw arcs from A and B (both sides). Connect the two intersection points — this is the perpendicular bisector. ✓

Construct an angle of 60° using only a compass and ruler (no protractor). Name the angle.
Medium
Answer

Draw ray OA. With same radius, mark arc from O (hits OA at B). Mark another arc from B with same radius to get C. ∠AOC = 60°

Bisect an angle of 90° to construct an angle of 45°. Describe each step.
Medium
Answer

First construct 90° using perpendicular. Then bisect it: draw arcs from both arms to find midpoint direction. The bisector makes 45° with each arm.

Construct triangle ABC with AB = 5 cm, BC = 6 cm, AC = 7 cm. Measure and name each angle type.
Medium
Answer

Draw BC=6cm. Arc of 5cm from B and 7cm from C. Intersection = A. Measure angles — all should be acute (it's an acute scalene triangle).

Construct an equilateral triangle with side 4 cm. What is each angle? Verify using a protractor.
Medium
Answer

Draw AB=4cm. Arcs of 4cm from A and B intersect at C. All sides = 4cm, all angles = 60°

Construct a right-angled triangle with legs 3 cm and 4 cm. Measure the hypotenuse — does it equal 5 cm?
Medium
Answer

Draw a right angle. Mark one leg = 3cm, other = 4cm. Hypotenuse should measure exactly 5 cm (3-4-5 Pythagorean triple ✓)

Construct triangle PQR where ∠P = 60°, ∠Q = 70°, and PQ = 5 cm. Find ∠R and verify.
Hard
Answer

∠R = 180° − 60° − 70° = 50°. Construct 60° at P and 70° at Q on segment PQ=5cm. The intersection gives R. Measure ∠R ≈ 50°.

Construct a rectangle ABCD with AB = 6 cm and BC = 4 cm using a compass and ruler only (no protractor). Show all construction marks.
Hard
Answer

Draw AB=6cm. Construct perpendiculars at A and B. Mark D on perpendicular at A (4cm from A) and C on perpendicular at B (4cm from B). Join DC. Verify DC=6cm and diagonals are equal. ✓

Symmetry

🗓 Learning Plan (2 weeks)

Week 1 – Line Symmetry
  • What is symmetry?
  • Lines of symmetry in shapes
  • Symmetry in alphabets
  • Drawing symmetric figures
Week 2 – Rotational Symmetry
  • Rotation and centre of rotation
  • Order of rotational symmetry
  • Symmetry in regular polygons
  • Symmetry in art and nature (mandalas, snowflakes)
Tip: Fold a paper in half — any shape that matches perfectly has a line of symmetry along the fold!

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

How many lines of symmetry does a square have?
Easy
Answer

4 (2 across sides, 2 across diagonals)

Which capital letters have at least one line of symmetry? (Think: A, B, C, D, E, H, M, T, U, W)
Easy
Answer

A, B, C, D, E, H, M, T, U, W, V, Y (among others). H, I, O, X have both horizontal and vertical lines.

Does a rectangle have rotational symmetry? If so, what is its order?
Easy
Answer

Yes. Order = 2 (looks the same at 180° and 360°)

What is the order of rotational symmetry for a regular hexagon?
Easy
Answer

Order = 6 (looks the same every 60°)

Draw the line(s) of symmetry for an equilateral triangle. How many are there?
Medium
Answer

3 lines — each goes from a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.

Complete the figure so that the dotted vertical line is its line of symmetry. (Given: left half of a star shape.)
Medium
Answer

Mirror each point of the left half across the axis. The right half is an exact reflection. ✓

A shape has rotational symmetry of order 4. What angles of rotation map it onto itself?
Medium
Answer

360°÷4 = 90°. Rotations: 90°, 180°, 270°, 360°

Name a shape that has rotational symmetry but NO line of symmetry. Explain.
Medium
Answer

A parallelogram (not a rhombus/rectangle). It has order-2 rotational symmetry but no line of symmetry.

Create a design that has exactly 3 lines of symmetry. What is its order of rotational symmetry?
Hard
Answer

An equilateral triangle or any regular 3-sided pattern has 3 lines of symmetry and order 3 rotational symmetry (120° each time).

A kaleidoscope creates a pattern with 8-fold rotational symmetry. (a) What angle separates each repeated unit? (b) How many lines of symmetry does the pattern have?
Hard
Answer

(a) 360°÷8 = 45° between each unit. (b) A pattern with order-8 rotational symmetry has 8 lines of symmetry.

✏️ Practice Tests (Simple → Complex)

Mark these on a number line: −4, −1, 0, 2, 5
Easy
Answer

←—(−4)—(−1)—(0)—(2)—(5)—→ in order from left to right. ✓

Which is greater: −7 or −3?
