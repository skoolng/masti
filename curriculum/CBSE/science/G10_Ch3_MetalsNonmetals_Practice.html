How does the galvanic (electrochemical) reaction between dissimilar metals in seawater relate to metal-water reactions?
Hard
Answer

When two different metals are in contact in an electrolyte (seawater), a galvanic cell forms: more reactive metal (anode) oxidises → loses electrons → corrodes faster. Less reactive (cathode) is protected. Example: iron bolt + copper plate in seawater → iron corrodes rapidly. Application: cathodic protection — zinc sacrificial anode protects steel ships. Criterion D: electrochemistry in marine engineering.

Explain why ionic compounds have high melting points.
Medium
Answer

Why does NaCl conduct electricity when dissolved in water but not when solid?
Medium
Answer

Solid: Na⁺ and Cl⁻ are fixed in the lattice → cannot move → no conduction. Dissolved: water molecules surround and separate the ions (hydration) → free mobile Na⁺ and Cl⁻ → can carry charge → conducts.
