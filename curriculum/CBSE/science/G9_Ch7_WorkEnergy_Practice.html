Work Definition — W = Fd cosθ

📺 Recommended Videos

Khan Academy Introduction to work and energy Work done by a constant force
CrashCourse Work, Energy & Power

Set A Recall & Concept

Define work in physics. How is it different from the everyday meaning of the word? Easy
Show Answer

In physics, work is done when a force causes displacement in the direction of the force. Everyday "work" can mean any effort, but in physics no work is done if there is no displacement or if force is perpendicular to motion.

Write the formula for work and state the SI unit. Easy
Show Answer

W = F × d × cosθ, where F is force (N), d is displacement (m), and θ is the angle between force and displacement. SI unit: joule (J); 1 J = 1 N·m.

A person pushes a wall with 50 N for 10 seconds but the wall does not move. How much work is done? Easy
Show Answer

Zero joules. Work requires displacement. Since the wall does not move, displacement = 0, so W = F × 0 = 0 J.

Why is no work done by a person holding a heavy bag while walking horizontally? Medium
Show Answer

The holding force is vertical (upward) while the displacement is horizontal. The angle θ = 90°, and cos 90° = 0, so W = Fd × 0 = 0 J.

State two conditions that must be satisfied for work to be done on an object. Easy
Show Answer

1) A net force must act on the object. 2) The object must undergo displacement with a component in the direction of the force.

Calculate the work done when a force of 20 N moves an object 5 m in the direction of the force. Easy
Show Answer

W = F × d = 20 N × 5 m = 100 J.

A force of 30 N acts at 60° to the direction of motion over 4 m. Calculate the work done. Medium
Show Answer

W = Fd cosθ = 30 × 4 × cos 60° = 120 × 0.5 = 60 J.

Explain why gravity does negative work when a ball is thrown upward. Medium
Show Answer

Gravity acts downward but displacement is upward, so θ = 180°. cos 180° = −1, giving W = Fd(−1) = negative work. The gravitational force opposes the motion.

Set B Application & Calculation

A student lifts a 5 kg book 1.2 m off the floor. Calculate the work done against gravity. (g = 10 m/s²) Easy
Show Answer

W = mgh = 5 × 10 × 1.2 = 60 J.

A horse pulls a cart with 400 N of force at 30° to the horizontal over 200 m. Find the work done. Medium
Show Answer

W = Fd cosθ = 400 × 200 × cos 30° = 80 000 × 0.866 ≈ 69 280 J ≈ 69.3 kJ.

A friction force of 15 N acts on a box sliding 3 m across a floor. What work does friction do? Easy
Show Answer

W = −Fd = −15 × 3 = −45 J. Friction acts opposite to displacement, so work is negative.

Two students push a 120 kg sofa 6 m. Student A pushes 80 N and Student B pushes 60 N, both horizontally. What is the total work done? Medium
Show Answer

Total force = 80 + 60 = 140 N. W = 140 × 6 = 840 J.

Calculate the work done by a 1500 N engine force moving a car 2 km along a straight road. Easy
Show Answer

W = F × d = 1500 × 2000 = 3 000 000 J = 3 MJ.

A 10 N force is applied at 45° to move a block 8 m. Compare this to the work done if the same force were applied horizontally. Medium
Show Answer

At 45°: W = 10 × 8 × cos 45° = 80 × 0.707 ≈ 56.6 J. Horizontally: W = 10 × 8 = 80 J. The angled force does about 29% less work.

A box is pulled 5 m up a frictionless incline by a 50 N force parallel to the slope. A 30 N friction force acts down the slope. Find the net work done on the box. Hard
Show Answer

Work by applied force = 50 × 5 = 250 J. Work by friction = −30 × 5 = −150 J. Net work = 250 − 150 = 100 J.

If 500 J of work is done moving an object 25 m, what is the magnitude of the applied force (assuming θ = 0°)? Easy
Show Answer

W = Fd → F = W/d = 500/25 = 20 N.

Set C Critical Thinking & Extended Response

A satellite orbits Earth in a circular path at constant speed. Does gravity do work on the satellite? Justify using W = Fd cosθ. Hard
Show Answer

No. For circular orbit, gravity always points toward the centre (inward) while displacement is always tangential (perpendicular to gravity). θ = 90°, cos 90° = 0, so W = 0 J. Speed stays constant, confirming zero net work.

Explain why it is harder to do work against friction on rough surfaces than smooth ones, using the work formula. Medium
Show Answer

Friction force F_f = μN. A rough surface has a higher coefficient of friction μ, so F_f is larger. Since W = F_f × d, more work must be done to overcome the greater friction force over the same displacement.

Design a scenario where force is applied but zero work is done. Identify the physical reason. Medium
Show Answer

Example: A waiter carries a tray horizontally across a room. The upward holding force is perpendicular to horizontal displacement (θ = 90°, cosθ = 0), so W = 0 J despite clear physical effort.

A 70 kg person walks up a staircase with 20 steps, each 15 cm high. Calculate total work done against gravity. (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

Total height h = 20 × 0.15 = 3 m. W = mgh = 70 × 10 × 3 = 2100 J.

Compare the work done by gravity on a 2 kg ball falling freely 5 m versus the same ball being lowered slowly by a rope 5 m. Is the work different? Hard
Show Answer

Work by gravity = mgh = 2 × 10 × 5 = 100 J in both cases. Gravity's work depends only on vertical displacement, not the path or speed. The tension in the rope does negative work when lowering slowly, but this does not change gravity's work.

Why do engineers care about the angle of an applied force? Give a practical example. Medium
Show Answer

Only the component of force along the displacement does useful work (F cosθ). A tractor pulling a plough at a low angle is more efficient than a steep angle because more force contributes to horizontal motion. Engineers minimise θ to maximise useful work.

A crane lifts a 500 kg load 30 m then moves it 20 m horizontally. Calculate total work done by the crane motor. (g = 10 m/s²) Hard
Show Answer

Work lifting = mgh = 500 × 10 × 30 = 150 000 J. Work moving horizontally (gravity perpendicular to horizontal displacement) = 0 J against gravity. Total useful work against gravity = 150 000 J = 150 kJ.

A force of 60 N is applied at 30° to drag a 10 kg box 12 m across a floor with friction coefficient 0.2. Find (a) work by applied force, (b) work by friction, (c) net work. (g = 10 m/s²) Hard
Show Answer

(a) W_app = 60 × 12 × cos 30° = 720 × 0.866 ≈ 623 J. (b) Normal force N = mg − F sinθ = 100 − 60 × 0.5 = 70 N. Friction = 0.2 × 70 = 14 N. W_fric = −14 × 12 = −168 J. (c) Net work ≈ 623 − 168 = 455 J.

Types of Energy — KE = ½mv², GPE = mgh

📺 Recommended Videos

Khan Academy Kinetic energy review Gravitational potential energy
CrashCourse Potential & Kinetic Energy

Set A Recall & Concept

List six different forms of energy studied in this chapter. Easy
Show Answer

Kinetic, gravitational potential, elastic potential, chemical, thermal (heat), electrical, nuclear (any six from this list).

Write the formula for kinetic energy and define each symbol. Easy
Show Answer

KE = ½mv², where m = mass (kg) and v = speed (m/s). KE is measured in joules (J).

Write the formula for gravitational potential energy and explain when it is used. Easy
Show Answer

GPE = mgh, where m = mass (kg), g = 10 m/s² on Earth, h = height above reference point (m). Used when an object is lifted above a reference level.

Where is the chemical energy stored in food and fuels? Easy
Show Answer

Chemical energy is stored in the bonds between atoms and molecules. When bonds break during reactions (e.g., combustion, digestion), energy is released as heat and light.

Define elastic potential energy and give two everyday examples. Easy
Show Answer

Elastic potential energy is energy stored in a stretched or compressed elastic object. Examples: stretched rubber band, compressed spring in a pen.

How does doubling the speed of a moving car affect its kinetic energy? Medium
Show Answer

KE = ½mv². If speed doubles (2v): KE = ½m(2v)² = ½m × 4v² = 4 × ½mv². KE increases by a factor of 4 (quadruples).

State the difference between thermal energy and temperature. Medium
Show Answer

Temperature measures the average kinetic energy of particles. Thermal energy is the total internal kinetic and potential energy of all particles. A large cool lake has more thermal energy than a small hot cup of tea, despite its lower temperature.

Name the energy transformations when a battery-powered torch is switched on. Easy
Show Answer

Chemical energy (battery) → Electrical energy → Light energy + Thermal energy (in bulb).

Set B Application & Calculation

Calculate the KE of a 1200 kg car travelling at 20 m/s. Easy
Show Answer

KE = ½mv² = ½ × 1200 × 400 = 240 000 J = 240 kJ.

A 5 kg ball is raised 8 m above the ground. Calculate its GPE. (g = 10 m/s²) Easy
Show Answer

GPE = mgh = 5 × 10 × 8 = 400 J.

A 0.2 kg arrow moves at 50 m/s. Find its kinetic energy. Easy
Show Answer

KE = ½ × 0.2 × 50² = 0.1 × 2500 = 250 J.

How fast must a 2 kg ball move to have 100 J of kinetic energy? Medium
Show Answer

KE = ½mv² → v² = 2KE/m = 200/2 = 100 → v = 10 m/s.

A 50 kg climber is 200 m above base camp. Calculate her GPE relative to base camp. (g = 10 m/s²) Easy
Show Answer

GPE = mgh = 50 × 10 × 200 = 100 000 J = 100 kJ.

Compare the KE of a 3 kg object at 4 m/s with that of a 12 kg object at 2 m/s. Medium
Show Answer

KE&sub1; = ½ × 3 × 16 = 24 J. KE&sub2; = ½ × 12 × 4 = 24 J. Both objects have identical kinetic energies of 24 J.

A spring stores 45 J of elastic PE. If it launches a 0.5 kg ball, what is the ball's maximum speed (assuming all energy converts to KE)? Medium
Show Answer

½mv² = 45 → v² = 90/0.5 = 180 → v = √180 ≈ 13.4 m/s.

At what height would a 10 kg object need to be for its GPE to equal the KE of a 2 kg object moving at 15 m/s? (g = 10 m/s²) Hard
Show Answer

KE = ½ × 2 × 225 = 225 J. GPE = mgh → h = GPE/(mg) = 225/(10 × 10) = 2.25 m.

Set C Critical Thinking & Extended Response

Why do car safety engineers care more about speed than mass when designing crumple zones? Use the KE formula. Hard
Show Answer

KE = ½mv². Speed is squared, so it has a greater impact: doubling speed quadruples KE, while doubling mass only doubles KE. Engineers must dissipate this larger energy in crashes, making speed a more critical safety factor.

Trace all energy transformations from sunlight to a moving bicycle with a solar-charged electric motor. Medium
Show Answer

Solar (radiant) energy → Electrical energy (solar cell) → Chemical energy (battery storage) → Electrical energy (motor) → Kinetic energy (bicycle motion) + Thermal energy (motor heat loss).

A 60 kg diver stands on a 10 m platform. Calculate GPE, then state what happens to that energy when she hits the water. (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

GPE = 60 × 10 × 10 = 6000 J. As she dives, GPE converts to KE. Upon hitting water, KE converts to sound energy, wave energy, and thermal energy (water warms slightly).

Trace energy conversions from nuclear fuel to electrical energy used in a home. Hard
Show Answer

Nuclear energy (fission in reactor) → Thermal energy (heats water) → Kinetic energy (steam spins turbine) → Kinetic energy (generator rotation) → Electrical energy transmitted to home.

Explain why a rolling ball gradually slows down on a flat surface in terms of energy types. Medium
Show Answer

The ball's KE is gradually converted to thermal energy (heat) and sound energy by friction between ball and surface, and air resistance. No energy is created or destroyed; it changes form.

A 1500 kg car brakes from 30 m/s to rest. How much thermal energy is produced in the brakes? Medium
Show Answer

KE lost = ½ × 1500 × 30² = 750 × 900 = 675 000 J = 675 kJ. All this KE converts to thermal energy in the brakes and tyres.

Explain the energy transformations in a hydroelectric dam from rainfall to light in a home. Hard
Show Answer

Solar energy evaporates water → Water vapour rises (GPE increases) → Rain fills reservoir (GPE stored) → Water flows down (GPE → KE) → Turbine spins (KE → KE of generator) → Electricity generated → Light in home.

Two identical balls are thrown horizontally from the same height: one at 10 m/s and another at 20 m/s. Compare their KE and GPE at launch. When do their GPEs become equal again? Hard
Show Answer

At launch, both have equal GPE (same height, same mass). KE&sub1; = ½m(100) = 50m J; KE&sub2; = ½m(400) = 200m J; Ball 2 has 4× the KE. Their GPEs become equal again when they reach the same height — which is the ground (h = 0, GPE = 0 for both).

Conservation of Energy

📺 Recommended Videos

Khan Academy Conservation of energy Pendulum energy conversion
CrashCourse Conservation of Energy explained

Set A Recall & Concept

State the Law of Conservation of Energy. Easy
Show Answer

Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only be converted from one form to another. The total energy in an isolated system remains constant.

Write the energy equation for a falling object (ignoring air resistance). Easy
Show Answer

mgh = ½mv² (GPE at top = KE at bottom). GPE converts entirely to KE during free fall.

At what point during a falling object's journey is its GPE maximum and its KE maximum? Easy
Show Answer

GPE is maximum at the highest point (before it falls). KE is maximum at the lowest point (just before hitting the ground) when all GPE has been converted.

Write the formula Q = mcΔT and explain each term. Easy
Show Answer

Q = heat energy (J), m = mass (kg), c = specific heat capacity (J/kg·°C), ΔT = change in temperature (°C). Used to calculate thermal energy absorbed or released.

A pendulum swings back and forth. Where does it have maximum KE and maximum GPE? Easy
Show Answer

Maximum GPE is at the two extreme ends of the swing (highest points). Maximum KE is at the bottom of the swing (lowest point). Energy continuously converts between these forms.

If no energy is created or destroyed, why does a bouncing ball eventually stop? Medium
Show Answer

Energy is not destroyed — it converts to thermal energy (ball and floor warm slightly) and sound energy on each bounce. The ball's mechanical energy dissipates into these forms, so it bounces lower each time until all mechanical energy is gone.

Explain how energy is conserved in a compressed spring launched vertically. Medium
Show Answer

Elastic PE (stored in spring) → KE (as object launches) → GPE (as object rises and slows). At maximum height, all energy is GPE again (assuming no air resistance).

What happens to the total mechanical energy of an object sliding down a frictionless ramp? Easy
Show Answer

Total mechanical energy (KE + GPE) remains constant. GPE decreases as height decreases, and KE increases by the same amount. No energy is lost to friction.

Set B Application & Calculation

A 3 kg ball is dropped from a height of 20 m. What is its speed just before hitting the ground? (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

mgh = ½mv² → gh = ½v² → v² = 2 × 10 × 20 = 400 → v = 20 m/s.

A 0.5 kg ball rolls off a 5 m high table. Calculate its KE when it reaches the ground. (g = 10 m/s²) Easy
Show Answer

KE = GPE = mgh = 0.5 × 10 × 5 = 25 J.

A 2 kg object is launched upward with 200 J of KE. How high will it rise? (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

KE = GPE → 200 = mgh = 2 × 10 × h → h = 200/20 = 10 m.

Water of mass 2 kg is heated from 20°C to 70°C. Calculate the heat energy absorbed. (c_water = 4200 J/kg·°C) Medium
Show Answer

Q = mcΔT = 2 × 4200 × 50 = 420 000 J = 420 kJ.

A roller-coaster car (500 kg) starts from rest at 40 m height. Find its speed at a point 10 m high. (g = 10 m/s²) Hard
Show Answer

mgh&sub1; = mgh&sub2; + ½mv². mg(40−10) = ½mv². v² = 2 × 10 × 30 = 600. v = √600 ≈ 24.5 m/s.

100 g of iron (c = 450 J/kg·°C) is heated by 500 J. By how much does its temperature rise? Medium
Show Answer

ΔT = Q/(mc) = 500/(0.1 × 450) = 500/45 ≈ 11.1°C.

A pendulum bob has 80 J of GPE at its highest point. If 5 J is lost to air resistance, what is its total mechanical energy at the midpoint? Medium
Show Answer

Total mechanical energy at midpoint = 80 − 5 = 75 J (5 J dissipated as thermal/sound energy by air resistance).

A 1 kg ball is thrown upward at 14 m/s. Calculate (a) initial KE, (b) maximum height reached. (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

(a) KE = ½ × 1 × 196 = 98 J. (b) KE = GPE → 98 = mgh → h = 98/10 = 9.8 m.

Set C Critical Thinking & Extended Response

A perpetual motion machine claims to produce more energy than it uses. Explain why this is impossible using the Law of Conservation of Energy. Hard
Show Answer

Conservation of energy states energy cannot be created. Any machine must lose some energy to friction and heat. A machine outputting more than input would be creating energy from nothing — physically impossible.

Describe all energy transformations in a hydroelectric turbine from stored water to electricity. Medium
Show Answer

GPE (stored water at height) → KE (falling water) → KE (spinning turbine blades) → Electrical energy (generator) + Thermal energy (friction in bearings).

A 2 kg stone is dropped from 45 m. Using energy conservation, find the speed at 20 m above ground. (g = 10 m/s²) Hard
Show Answer

Total energy at top = mgh = 2 × 10 × 45 = 900 J. At 20 m: GPE = 2×10×20 = 400 J. KE = 900−400 = 500 J. ½mv² = 500 → v² = 500 → v ≈ 22.4 m/s.

Why is the equation mgh = ½mv² only valid without air resistance? What modification is needed in real life? Hard
Show Answer

With friction/air resistance, some mechanical energy converts to thermal/sound energy. The correct equation becomes: mgh = ½mv² + W_friction. The friction work term accounts for energy dissipated, so the object has less KE than the frictionless equation predicts.

A skateboarder (60 kg) descends from a 4 m ramp and reaches the bottom at 8 m/s. How much energy was lost to friction? (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

Initial GPE = 60 × 10 × 4 = 2400 J. Final KE = ½ × 60 × 64 = 1920 J. Energy lost = 2400 − 1920 = 480 J (to friction and air resistance).

Explain the energy changes in a nuclear power plant from nuclear fuel to heat energy in a home radiator. Hard
Show Answer

Nuclear energy (fission) → Thermal energy (heats coolant) → Thermal energy (steam) → Mechanical KE (turbine) → Electrical energy (generator) → Electrical energy (transmission) → Thermal energy (home radiator).

Calculate the temperature rise if a 70 kg person's KE while running at 5 m/s were entirely converted to heating their body. (c_body ≈ 3500 J/kg·°C) Hard
Show Answer

KE = ½ × 70 × 25 = 875 J. ΔT = Q/(mc) = 875/(70 × 3500) = 875/245 000 ≈ 0.004°C. Extremely small — the body cannot convert motion energy to significant warming.

Two springs store 60 J and 40 J of elastic PE. They are attached to the same 0.5 kg block and released simultaneously. What is the block's maximum speed? Hard
Show Answer

Total elastic PE = 60 + 40 = 100 J. ½mv² = 100 → v² = 200/0.5 = 400 → v = 20 m/s.

Power and Efficiency

📺 Recommended Videos

Khan Academy Power in physics
CrashCourse Efficiency and Power

Set A Recall & Concept

Define power in physics. State its formula and SI unit. Easy
Show Answer

Power is the rate of doing work (or transferring energy). P = W/t, where W = work done (J) and t = time (s). SI unit: watt (W); 1 W = 1 J/s.

Write the alternative formula for power using force and velocity. Easy
Show Answer

P = Fv, where F = force (N) and v = velocity (m/s). Used when an object moves at constant speed.

Define efficiency of a machine and write its formula. Easy
Show Answer

Efficiency = (useful energy output / total energy input) × 100%. It tells what percentage of input energy is converted to the desired output.

Why is the efficiency of a real machine always less than 100%? Easy
Show Answer

Real machines always lose some energy to friction, air resistance, sound, or heat. This wasted energy means useful output is always less than total input, so efficiency never reaches 100%.

A 1000 W motor and a 200 W motor both do the same amount of work. Which takes longer? Easy
Show Answer

The 200 W motor takes longer. Since P = W/t, t = W/P. Lower power means more time for the same work.

Convert 5 kW to watts and calculate the work done in 30 seconds. Easy
Show Answer

5 kW = 5000 W. W = Pt = 5000 × 30 = 150 000 J = 150 kJ.

A machine has 80% efficiency. If 500 J is input, how much useful work is done? Easy
Show Answer

Useful output = 0.80 × 500 = 400 J. The remaining 100 J is wasted (mainly as heat).

Explain the difference between a powerful machine and an efficient machine. Medium
Show Answer

A powerful machine does work quickly (high W/t). An efficient machine wastes little energy (high useful output/input ratio). A machine can be powerful but inefficient (fast but wastes much energy), or efficient but low-powered (converts energy well but slowly).

Set B Application & Calculation

A motor does 12 000 J of work in 4 minutes. Calculate its power output. Easy
Show Answer

P = W/t = 12 000/(4 × 60) = 12 000/240 = 50 W.

A car engine exerts 3000 N at 20 m/s. Calculate the engine's power. Easy
Show Answer

P = Fv = 3000 × 20 = 60 000 W = 60 kW.

An electric motor uses 2000 J of electrical energy and produces 1600 J of useful mechanical energy. Calculate its efficiency. Easy
Show Answer

Efficiency = (1600/2000) × 100% = 80%.

A 60 kg athlete runs up stairs (height 10 m) in 8 seconds. Calculate her power output. (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

W = mgh = 60 × 10 × 10 = 6000 J. P = W/t = 6000/8 = 750 W.

A light bulb is 12% efficient. How much electrical energy is needed to produce 60 J of light? Medium
Show Answer

Input = useful output / efficiency = 60/0.12 = 500 J.

A crane lifts 800 kg to 15 m in 20 s. Calculate power. How does this change if it takes 40 s? (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

W = 800 × 10 × 15 = 120 000 J. P&sub1; = 120 000/20 = 6000 W = 6 kW. P&sub2; = 120 000/40 = 3000 W = 3 kW. Taking twice as long halves the power.

A pump has 75% efficiency and uses 4000 W. What is its useful power output? Medium
Show Answer

Useful power = 0.75 × 4000 = 3000 W = 3 kW.

A petrol engine inputs 50 MJ of chemical energy and produces 12.5 MJ of mechanical work. Calculate efficiency and wasted energy. Medium
Show Answer

Efficiency = (12.5/50) × 100% = 25%. Wasted energy = 50 − 12.5 = 37.5 MJ (released as heat and sound).

Set C Critical Thinking & Extended Response

A coal power plant is 35% efficient. If it burns coal releasing 100 MJ, how much electrical energy is produced and how much is wasted? Medium
Show Answer

Electrical output = 0.35 × 100 = 35 MJ. Wasted = 65 MJ (as heat in cooling towers, exhaust gases, friction losses).

Compare the efficiency of a coal plant (35%), gas turbine (55%), and solar panel (22%). Discuss implications for energy policy. Hard
Show Answer

Gas turbines convert more fuel energy to electricity. However, solar panels use free sunlight, so their 22% efficiency still produces clean electricity at low running cost. Policy should consider both efficiency and environmental impact — renewable sources with lower efficiency may be preferable to high-efficiency fossil fuel plants.

Why do engineers try to reduce friction in machines? Use efficiency to explain. Medium
Show Answer

Friction converts useful mechanical energy to waste heat, reducing useful output. Less friction means less energy wasted, increasing efficiency (useful output/input × 100%). This reduces fuel costs and environmental impact.

A 500 W motor lifts a 50 kg box 4 m. How long does it take? What is the efficiency if 10% is lost to friction? (g = 10 m/s²) Hard
Show Answer

Useful work = mgh = 50×10×4 = 2000 J. Time = W/P = 2000/500 = 4 s. With 10% friction loss: total input needed = 2000/0.9 = 2222 J. Efficiency = 2000/2222 × 100% = 90%.

An LED bulb produces 800 lumens at 10 W. A filament bulb produces the same lumens at 60 W. Compare annual cost savings at 10 hours/day at ₹8/kWh. Hard
Show Answer

Annual energy: LED = 10 × 10 × 365 = 36 500 Wh = 36.5 kWh; Filament = 60 × 10 × 365 = 219 kWh. Savings = 182.5 kWh × ₹8 = ₹1460/year per bulb. LED is 6× more efficient.

Explain the Sankey diagram concept. Describe what one would look like for a 40% efficient car engine. Hard
Show Answer

A Sankey diagram uses arrow widths proportional to energy amounts. For a 40% efficient engine: input arrow (100%) splits into useful mechanical work (40%, narrow arrow rightward) and waste heat (60%, wide arrow downward). The wide waste arrow shows most energy is dissipated as heat through exhaust and friction.

Two students race up stairs: Student A (60 kg) climbs 12 m in 10 s; Student B (80 kg) climbs 10 m in 8 s. Who is more powerful? (g = 10 m/s²) Medium
Show Answer

P_A = mgh/t = 60×10×12/10 = 720 W. P_B = 80×10×10/8 = 1000 W. Student B is more powerful (1000 W vs 720 W).

A heat pump moves 4 kJ of heat energy into a room using only 1 kJ of electrical energy. Calculate its COP and explain why this does not violate energy conservation. Hard
Show Answer

COP = heat delivered/work input = 4/1 = 4. This does NOT violate conservation of energy because the pump moves heat from outside air (3 kJ) plus uses electrical work (1 kJ) to deliver 4 kJ total. It does not create energy — it transfers it from outside.

Simple Machines — Levers, Pulleys, Inclined Planes

📺 Recommended Videos

Khan Academy Simple machines overview Mechanical advantage
CrashCourse Simple Machines

Set A Recall & Concept

List five types of simple machines and give one everyday example of each. Easy
Show Answer

1. Lever – seesaw. 2. Pulley – flagpole. 3. Inclined plane – ramp. 4. Wheel and axle – doorknob. 5. Screw – jar lid. 6. Wedge – axe (any five).

Define mechanical advantage (MA) and write its formula. Easy
Show Answer

MA = Load force / Effort force = F_load / F_effort. MA > 1 means the machine multiplies force. Also expressed as distance ratio: MA = effort distance / load distance.

Name and describe the three classes of levers. Give one example of each. Medium
Show Answer

Class 1: Fulcrum between effort and load (e.g., seesaw, scissors). Class 2: Load between fulcrum and effort (e.g., wheelbarrow, nutcracker). Class 3: Effort between fulcrum and load (e.g., tweezers, fishing rod).

In a class 2 lever, where is the load relative to the fulcrum and effort? Easy
Show Answer

In a class 2 lever, the load is between the fulcrum and the effort. This always gives MA > 1 (force multiplication). Example: wheelbarrow (wheel = fulcrum, load in middle, hands apply effort at handles).

How does an inclined plane reduce the effort needed to move a load upward? Easy
Show Answer

An inclined plane spreads the vertical height gain over a longer sloped distance. The effort needed equals the component F sinθ instead of the full weight. Trade-off: less force required but over a greater distance.

How does a block and tackle pulley system increase mechanical advantage? Medium
Show Answer

A block and tackle uses multiple pulleys; each additional supporting rope segment reduces the effort needed. MA = number of rope segments supporting the load.

Why can simple machines not reduce the total work done? Medium
Show Answer

By conservation of energy, Work input = Work output (ideal machine). If MA > 1 (less effort), effort must be applied over a greater distance. Force × distance remains constant — machines trade force for distance.

Classify these as Class 1, 2, or 3 levers: (a) human forearm lifting a weight (b) crowbar (c) wheelbarrow. Medium
Show Answer

(a) Human forearm: Class 3 (effort at bicep between elbow fulcrum and load in hand). (b) Crowbar: Class 1 (fulcrum between effort and load). (c) Wheelbarrow: Class 2 (load between wheel fulcrum and handles).

Set B Application & Calculation

A lever has effort arm 2 m and load arm 0.5 m. Calculate MA and the effort needed to lift a 200 N load. Easy
Show Answer

MA = effort arm / load arm = 2/0.5 = 4. Effort = Load/MA = 200/4 = 50 N.

A ramp is 6 m long and 1.5 m high. Calculate its MA. Easy
Show Answer

MA = ramp length / height = 6/1.5 = 4. Only ¼ the force is needed compared to lifting directly.

A block and tackle has 5 rope segments supporting the load. What effort is needed to lift a 500 N load? Easy
Show Answer

MA = 5. Effort = 500/5 = 100 N.

A screwdriver (wheel and axle) has handle radius 2 cm and shaft radius 0.5 cm. Calculate its MA. Medium
Show Answer

MA = handle radius / shaft radius = 2/0.5 = 4. Four times more torque is applied to the screw than to the handle.

A 300 N load is moved up a ramp of length 10 m and height 2 m. Calculate (a) MA, (b) effort needed, (c) work done. Medium
Show Answer

(a) MA = 10/2 = 5. (b) Effort = 300/5 = 60 N. (c) Work = Effort × distance = 60 × 10 = 600 J = mgh = 300 × 2 = 600 J. ✓

A lever is 4 m long with fulcrum 1 m from the load. What effort is needed to lift a 120 N load? Medium
Show Answer

Load arm = 1 m. Effort arm = 4 − 1 = 3 m. MA = 3/1 = 3. Effort = 120/3 = 40 N.

(a) Effort = 600/4 = 150 N. (b) Rope pulled = 4 × 3 = 12 m. (c) Work in = 150 × 12 = 1800 J. Work out = 600 × 3 = 1800 J. ✓ Equal (ideal machine).

MA = length/thickness = 12/2 = 6. A sharp knife has a much smaller tip width → greater MA → the same force creates much higher cutting pressure. E.g., MA = 12/0.5 = 24 for a knife with 0.5 cm tip.

A real pulley has MA = 4 but efficiency = 75%. What effort is actually needed to lift a 400 N load? Hard
Show Answer

Ideal effort = 400/4 = 100 N. With 75% efficiency: Actual effort = 100/0.75 ≈ 133 N. More force is needed because friction in pulleys wastes energy.

Ancient Egyptians used ramps to build the pyramids. Estimate the MA of a ramp 300 m long rising 30 m. Explain why this was practical. Medium
Show Answer

MA = 300/30 = 10. Workers needed only 1/10th of the force to move each stone. With thousands of workers, this trade-off (more distance, less force) made the enormous task achievable.

Compare scissors (Class 1), tweezers (Class 3), and a wheelbarrow (Class 2) in terms of MA and the trade-off each makes. Hard
Show Answer

Scissors: Class 1, effort arm > load arm → MA > 1, multiplies force. Tweezers: Class 3, effort arm < load arm → MA < 1, sacrifices force for precision/range of motion. Wheelbarrow: Class 2, always MA > 1, amplifies force to lift heavy loads but reduces range of motion.

Design a compound machine using a lever and a pulley to lift a 1000 N load with only 50 N of effort. Specify the MA of each component. Hard
Show Answer

Total MA needed = 1000/50 = 20. Design: Lever MA = 4 (effort arm 4× load arm) + Pulley MA = 5 (5 rope segments). Combined MA = 4 × 5 = 20. Machines in series multiply their individual MAs.

Explain why a screw with finer threads has greater mechanical advantage than one with coarser threads. Medium
Show Answer

MA of screw = circumference of rotation / pitch (distance per turn). Finer threads mean smaller pitch → more turns to advance the same distance → effort applied over greater distance → higher MA. Fine-threaded screws grip better but require more turns.

A seesaw has a 40 kg child 2 m from the fulcrum. Where must a 60 kg adult sit to balance? Identify the lever class. Medium
Show Answer

For balance: F&sub1;d&sub1; = F&sub2;d&sub2; → 40×10×2 = 60×10×d → 800 = 600d → d = 800/600 ≈ 1.33 m from fulcrum. Class 1 lever (fulcrum between the two forces).

Why do surgeons use tweezers (Class 3 levers) despite their MA being less than 1? What advantage do they gain? Hard
Show Answer

Class 3 levers sacrifice force (MA < 1) but gain greater range and precision of movement at the tip. A small movement of the fingers produces a precise, controlled movement at the tip. Surgeons prioritize control and dexterity over force multiplication.

A car jack uses a screw with handle radius 30 cm and screw pitch 0.5 cm. Calculate MA. What force can it exert with 20 N applied? Why is this important for safety? Hard

MA = circumference/pitch = (2π × 30)/0.5 = 188.5/0.5 ≈ 377. Force output = 20 × 377 = 7540 N. This allows a person to safely lift a 1500+ kg car using modest hand force, demonstrating the importance of high-MA machines for safety applications.

How does the principle of energy conservation relate to the challenge of renewable energy storage? Why can't we just "make more energy"? Hard
Show Answer
