Set A Concept Check — Similar Figures Basics

Define similar figures. What two conditions must be satisfied for two polygons to be similar?
Show Answer

Two polygons are similar if: (1) All corresponding angles are equal, and (2) All corresponding sides are proportional (same ratio). Symbol: ~

Easy
Are all circles similar? Justify your answer.
Show Answer

Yes. All circles have the same shape. Any circle can be obtained from another by scaling (they all have the same angle measure — 360° — and sides proportional by ratio of radii). Hence all circles are similar.

Easy
Are all squares similar? Are all rectangles similar? Explain the difference.
Show Answer

All squares are similar (all angles are 90°, all sides equal so ratio is constant). NOT all rectangles are similar — a 2×4 rectangle and a 3×4 rectangle have different aspect ratios, so sides are not proportional.

Easy
Two similar triangles have a scale factor of 3:5. If the perimeter of the smaller triangle is 24 cm, find the perimeter of the larger triangle.
Show Answer

Scale factor = 3:5. Perimeters are in the same ratio as sides.
Perimeter of larger = 24 × (5/3) = 40 cm

Easy
Triangle ABC ~ Triangle PQR. Write all three pairs of equal angles and all three pairs of proportional sides.
Show Answer

Equal angles: ∠A = ∠P, ∠B = ∠Q, ∠C = ∠R
Proportional sides: AB/PQ = BC/QR = CA/RP

Easy
If △DEF ~ △XYZ with DE = 6, EF = 8, DF = 10 and XY = 9, find YZ and XZ.
Show Answer

Scale factor = XY/DE = 9/6 = 3/2
YZ = EF × (3/2) = 8 × 1.5 = 12
XZ = DF × (3/2) = 10 × 1.5 = 15

Medium
Explain: "Congruent figures are always similar but similar figures are not always congruent."
Show Answer

Congruent figures have equal angles AND equal sides (scale factor = 1), so they automatically satisfy similarity conditions. Similar figures only require proportional sides — scale factor can be anything, so they may differ in size and thus not be congruent.

Medium
Two similar pentagons have corresponding sides 4 cm and 6 cm. Find the ratio of their perimeters.
Show Answer

Ratio of perimeters = ratio of corresponding sides = 4:6 = 2:3

Medium
A photograph is enlarged such that a 4 cm length becomes 10 cm. Is this an example of similar figures? What is the scale factor?
Show Answer

Yes — enlargement preserves shape (all angles same), and all lengths scale by the same factor. Scale factor = 10/4 = 5/2 or 2.5

Medium
Can two triangles with angles 40°, 60°, 80° and sides 3, 4, 5 cm be similar to a triangle with angles 40°, 60°, 80° and sides 6, 8, 10 cm? Verify the side ratios.
Show Answer

Angles are identical ✓. Side ratios: 6/3 = 8/4 = 10/5 = 2 ✓. All ratios equal, so yes, they are similar with scale factor 2.

YZ/MN = 4/3 → YZ = 12 × (4/3) = 16 cm

In similar triangles, three sides of △1 are x, x+2, x+4 and the corresponding sides of △2 are 6, 9, 12. Find x.

Ratio must be constant: x/6 = (x+2)/9 = (x+4)/12
From x/6 = (x+2)/9: 9x = 6x+12 → 3x = 12 → x = 4

4.5(2x−1) = 3(x+2) → 9x−4.5 = 3x+6 → 6x = 10.5 → x = 1.75

Scale factor = 26/13 = 2
DE = 5×2 = 10, EF = 12×2 = 24, DF = 26

Medium
Two similar figures have scale factor k. If a side of the smaller figure is s, write an expression for the corresponding side of the larger figure and for the ratio of perimeters.

Corresponding side of larger figure = ks
Ratio of perimeters = 1:k (smaller to larger)

Set C Identifying Similarity — Mixed Questions

State whether: all equilateral triangles are similar. Give reason.
Show Answer

True. All equilateral triangles have angles 60°-60°-60°. Since all corresponding angles are equal, all equilateral triangles are similar (by AA criterion).

Easy
△1 has angles 50°, 70°, 60°. △2 has angles 60°, 50°, 70°. Are they similar?
Show Answer

Both have the same set of angles {50°, 60°, 70°}. Yes, they are similar (corresponding angles can be matched).

Easy
A map is drawn to scale 1:50000. If the distance between two cities on the map is 3 cm, what is the actual distance?
Show Answer

Actual distance = 3 × 50000 = 150000 cm = 1.5 km

Easy
Given △ABC ~ △PQR, ∠A = 45°, ∠B = 75°. Find ∠R.
Show Answer

∠C = 180° − 45° − 75° = 60°. Since △ABC ~ △PQR, ∠R = ∠C = 60°

Easy
Two similar triangles have sides 6, 8, 10 and 9, 12, 15. Verify they are similar and state the scale factor.
Show Answer

Ratios: 9/6 = 1.5, 12/8 = 1.5, 15/10 = 1.5. All equal ✓
Scale factor = 3:2

Medium
△ABC ~ △DEF. ∠A = 3x+5°, ∠D = 5x−15°. Find x and ∠A.
Show Answer

∠A = ∠D (corresponding angles)
3x+5 = 5x−15 → 20 = 2x → x = 10
∠A = 3(10)+5 = 35°

Medium
Are right-angled isosceles triangles always similar? Justify.
Show Answer

A right-angled isosceles triangle always has angles 90°-45°-45°. Since all such triangles have identical angles, they are always similar by AA. Yes, always similar.

Medium
In △ABC, a line DE is drawn parallel to BC cutting AB at D and AC at E. If AD = 3, DB = 5, AE = 4.5, find EC.
Show Answer

By BPT: AD/DB = AE/EC → 3/5 = 4.5/EC
EC = 4.5 × 5/3 = 7.5

Medium
△ABC has AB = 6, BC = 8, AC = 10. △PQR has PQ = 9, QR = 12, PR = 15. Are they similar? Find the scale factor.
Show Answer

Ratios: 9/6 = 1.5, 12/8 = 1.5, 15/10 = 1.5. All equal. Yes, similar. Scale factor = 3:2

Hard

This is the ambiguous SSA case. Example: △1 with sides 5, 7 and angle 30° opposite the side of length 5, and △2 with same measurements but the angle 30° is between the two sides — different triangles can be formed that are not similar. SSA is not a valid similarity criterion.

Prove that if two triangles are similar, their corresponding medians are in the same ratio as their corresponding sides.
Show Answer

Same shape = equal angles, proportional sides (scale factor can vary). Same size = equal measurements. Similar figures: same shape only. Congruent figures: same shape AND same size (scale factor = 1).

Distance = 3 × 2 = 6 m

Set A Identifying the Correct Criterion

State the AA similarity criterion. How many angles do you actually need to verify?
Show Answer

AA: If two angles of one triangle are equal to two angles of another triangle, the triangles are similar. You only need 2 angles — the third follows automatically (angle sum = 180°).

Easy
State the SSS similarity criterion. How does it differ from the SSS congruence criterion?
Show Answer

SSS Similarity: If all three pairs of corresponding sides are proportional, the triangles are similar. SSS Congruence: sides must be equal (not just proportional). Similarity uses ratios; congruence uses equality.

Easy
State the SAS similarity criterion. Which angle must be "included"?
Show Answer

SAS Similarity: If two pairs of corresponding sides are proportional AND the included angle (the angle between those two sides) is equal, the triangles are similar. The angle must be between the two proportional sides.

Easy
△ABC and △DEF: ∠A = ∠D = 40°, ∠B = ∠E = 80°. Which criterion applies?
Show Answer

AA criterion. Two pairs of equal angles are sufficient to establish similarity.

Easy
△ABC: AB = 4, BC = 6, AC = 8. △DEF: DE = 6, EF = 9, DF = 12. Which criterion applies?
Show Answer

Check ratios: DE/AB = 6/4 = 1.5, EF/BC = 9/6 = 1.5, DF/AC = 12/8 = 1.5. All equal. SSS criterion.

Easy
△ABC: AB = 3, AC = 5, ∠A = 60°. △DEF: DE = 6, DF = 10, ∠D = 60°. Which criterion applies?
Show Answer

AB/DE = 3/6 = 1/2, AC/DF = 5/10 = 1/2 (proportional), and included angle ∠A = ∠D = 60°. SAS criterion.

Medium
∠A = 70°, ∠C = 50° in △ABC. ∠P = 70°, ∠R = 60° in △PQR. Are the triangles similar? Explain.
Show Answer

∠B = 180−70−50 = 60°. ∠Q = 180−70−60 = 50°.
Angles of △ABC: 70°, 60°, 50°. Angles of △PQR: 70°, 60°, 50°. Same set — yes, similar by AA.

Medium
Can two triangles be similar by SSS if two sides are proportional but the third is not? Explain.
Show Answer

No. SSS similarity requires ALL THREE pairs of corresponding sides to be proportional. If even one pair has a different ratio, SSS similarity does not hold.

Medium
△ABC: AB = 5, BC = 7, ∠B = 45°. △PQR: PQ = 10, QR = 14, ∠Q = 45°. Identify the similarity criterion and write the similarity statement.
Show Answer

AB/PQ = 5/10 = 1/2, BC/QR = 7/14 = 1/2, ∠B = ∠Q = 45° (included angles). SAS criterion: △ABC ~ △PQR

Hard
Why is SSA (two sides and a non-included angle) NOT a valid similarity criterion?
Show Answer

With SSA, two different triangles can be constructed with the same two side ratios and the same non-included angle (the ambiguous case). Since the triangle is not uniquely determined, SSA cannot guarantee similarity.

Set A Direct Application of BPT

State the Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales' Theorem).
Show Answer

If a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle, it divides the other two sides proportionally. In △ABC, if DE ∥ BC, then AD/DB = AE/EC.

Easy
In △ABC, DE ∥ BC. AD = 4, DB = 6, AE = 5. Find EC.
Show Answer

By BPT: AD/DB = AE/EC → 4/6 = 5/EC → EC = 5×6/4 = 7.5

Easy
In △ABC, DE ∥ BC. AD = 3, AB = 9, AE = 4. Find EC.
