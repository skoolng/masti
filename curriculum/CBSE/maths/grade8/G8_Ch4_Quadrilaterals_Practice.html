Can a quadrilateral have three obtuse angles?
Easy
Show Answer

In quadrilateral ABCD, ∠A = 2∠B = 3∠C = 6∠D. Find all angles.
Medium
Show Answer

Prove the angle sum of a quadrilateral is 360° using the exterior angle method.

Prove using triangles: quadrilateral ABCD has angle sum 360°.

Hard

In quadrilateral ABCD, the diagonals bisect each other. What type must it be? Justify using angles.

In ABCD, ∠ABD = 30°, ∠BDC = 40°, ∠ADB = 50°, ∠DBC = 60°. Find ∠A+∠C.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: ∠B = ∠ABD+∠DBC=90°. ∠D=∠ADB+∠BDC=90°. ∠A+∠C=360−90−90=180°.

Prove that opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: In cyclic quadrilateral ABCD inscribed in circle: ∠A subtends arc BCD, ∠C subtends arc BAD. Together both arcs=full circle=360°. Since inscribed angle=half arc: ∠A+∠C=(1/2)(360°)=180°.

A quadrilateral has all angles different. Minimum possible largest angle?
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: If all different and largest minimised: distribute as evenly as possible. E.g., 89°,90°,91°,90° — but not all different. Try 88°,89°,90°,93° sum=360°. Largest=93°. With truly all different: e.g., 87°,89°,91°,93° sum=360°. Largest=93°. Minimum possible largest when spread evenly ≈90°+.

If the diagonals of quadrilateral ABCD meet at O, prove ∠AOB+∠COD = 180°−∠BOC or find the correct relationship.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: At point O, angles around a point sum to 360°. ∠AOB+∠BOC+∠COD+∠DOA=360°. Also ∠AOB=∠COD (vertically opposite) and ∠BOC=∠DOA. So 2∠AOB+2∠BOC=360° → ∠AOB+∠BOC=180°. ∠AOB and ∠BOC are supplementary.

Is every parallelogram a rectangle?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: No. A parallelogram only needs opposite sides parallel — angles need not be 90°.

Is every square a rhombus?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Yes. A square has all sides equal (rhombus property) AND all angles 90°.

Is every rhombus a square?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: No. A rhombus has all sides equal but angles need not be 90°.

What is the most specific name for a quadrilateral with all sides equal and all angles 90°?
Easy

Easy

What property distinguishes a trapezium from a parallelogram?

How is a kite different from a rhombus?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Kite: two pairs of adjacent equal sides (not all four equal). Rhombus: all four sides equal and opposite sides parallel.

Name a quadrilateral with no parallel sides.

Is a square a special kite? Explain with properties.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Yes. A square has AB=BC (adjacent) and CD=DA (adjacent). So it satisfies the kite definition (two pairs of adjacent equal sides). Also all sides equal, so both kite pairs are equal.

Prove that a parallelogram with equal diagonals is a rectangle.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: In parallelogram ABCD: AB∥CD, AB=CD. Diagonal AC=BD (given). △ABC≅△DCB (SSS: AB=DC, BC=BC, AC=DB). So ∠ABC=∠DCB. But they're co-interior (supplementary): 2∠ABC=180° → ∠ABC=90°. All angles 90° → rectangle.

Prove that a rhombus with equal diagonals is a square.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Rhombus has all sides equal. If diagonals equal, it satisfies both rhombus + rectangle conditions (by previous result). Rectangle with all sides equal = square. ✓

How many distinct types of quadrilaterals exist if we classify by number of equal sides: (0,1,2,3,4 equal sides)?
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: 0: general quad. 1 pair equal: kite (adjacent) or isosceles trap (opposite). 2 pairs equal (opposite): parallelogram. 2 pairs equal (adjacent): kite. 3: rare cases. 4: rhombus/square. Many subtypes exist.

A quadrilateral has perpendicular diagonals. Name all types that satisfy this.
Hard
Show Answer

An isosceles trapezium has equal non-parallel sides. Prove the base angles are equal.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Drop perpendiculars from parallel side vertices. Two congruent right triangles formed. Base angles of trapezium equal (corresponding angles in congruent triangles).

Can a quadrilateral have exactly two right angles? If so, what type?
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Yes. Example: right trapezium (two adjacent right angles). The other two angles sum to 180°, each can be non-right.

Show that a quadrilateral inscribed in a circle must have opposite angles summing to 180°.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Each angle is an inscribed angle subtending the opposite arc. ∠A subtends arc BCD, ∠C subtends arc BAD. Arc BCD + arc BAD = full circle=360°. So ∠A+∠C=(1/2)(360°)=180°.

Design a Venn diagram showing: Parallelograms, Rectangles, Rhombuses, Squares. State all set relationships.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Square ⊂ Rectangle ⊂ Parallelogram. Square ⊂ Rhombus ⊂ Parallelogram. Rectangle ∩ Rhombus = Square. All ⊂ Quadrilateral. Trapezium and Kite are separate sets (not ⊂ Parallelogram, in strict definition).

Define a parallelogram.
Easy

Answer: A parallelogram is a quadrilateral with both pairs of opposite sides parallel.

State three properties of a parallelogram.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: (1) Opposite sides are equal. (2) Opposite angles are equal. (3) Diagonals bisect each other.

In parallelogram ABCD, AB = 5 cm and BC = 3 cm. Find all side lengths.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: AB=CD=5 cm; BC=DA=3 cm. (Opposite sides equal.)

In parallelogram ABCD, ∠A = 70°. Find ∠B, ∠C, ∠D.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: ∠C=70° (opposite). ∠B=∠D=110° (co-interior with ∠A, so 180°−70°=110°).

Are the diagonals of a parallelogram equal?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Not necessarily. Diagonals bisect each other but are not equal in a general parallelogram. (Equal only in rectangle.)

In parallelogram ABCD, diagonals meet at O. AO = 5 cm. Find AC.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: AC = 2×AO = 10 cm. (Diagonals bisect each other.)

Find the area of a parallelogram with base 8 cm and height 5 cm.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Area = base × height = 8×5 = 40 cm².

What are co-interior (same-side interior) angles in a parallelogram?
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Adjacent angles in a parallelogram. They sum to 180° (supplementary).

In parallelogram ABCD, ∠A = (3x+10)° and ∠C = (5x−30)°. Find x.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: ∠A=∠C (opposite). 3x+10=5x−30 → 40=2x → x=20. ∠A=∠C=70°.

Is a parallelogram a trapezium? Explain.
Easy
Show Answer

Answer: Depends on definition. In the inclusive definition (trapezium = at least one pair of parallel sides), yes. In the exclusive definition (exactly one pair), no.

In parallelogram ABCD, ∠A = (2x+15)° and ∠B = (3x−10)°. Find all angles.
Medium
Show Answer

Prove: opposite sides of a parallelogram are equal.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: In ∥gm ABCD, draw diagonal AC. △ABC and △CDA: ∠BAC=∠DCA (alt. angles, AB∥DC), ∠BCA=∠DAC (alt. angles, BC∥AD), AC=CA. By ASA: △ABC≅△CDA. So AB=CD, BC=DA. ✓

Prove: the diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: In ∥gm ABCD, diagonals meet at O. △AOB and △COD: AB=CD (opp. sides), ∠OAB=∠OCD (alt. angles), ∠OBA=∠ODC (alt. angles). By ASA: △AOB≅△COD. So AO=CO, BO=DO. ✓

In parallelogram ABCD, diagonals AC and BD intersect at O. If AO=3x−1 and OC=x+5, find AC.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: AO=OC: 3x−1=x+5 → 2x=6 → x=3. AO=8, AC=16 cm.

Find the area of parallelogram with sides 10 cm, 6 cm and included angle 30°.
Medium
Show Answer

In parallelogram ABCD, AB=6 cm. A line through A parallel to BD meets DC at E. If DE=4 cm, find EC.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: DC=AB=6 cm. EC=DC−DE=6−4=2 cm.

A parallelogram has perimeter 36 cm. One side is 10 cm. Find all sides.

In ∥gm ABCD, ∠DAB = 2∠ABC. Find all angles.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: ∠DAB+∠ABC=180°. 2∠ABC+∠ABC=180° → 3∠ABC=180° → ∠ABC=60°. ∠DAB=120°. ∠BCD=60°, ∠CDA=120°.

If one angle of a parallelogram is 90°, prove all angles are 90°.
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: Let ∠A=90°. ∠B=180°−90°=90° (co-interior). ∠C=∠A=90° (opposite). ∠D=∠B=90°. All 90°. ✓

In parallelogram ABCD, M is midpoint of BC. AM is drawn. Area of △ABM : Area of ∥gm ABCD?
Medium
Show Answer

Answer: △ABM has base BM=BC/2, height = height of ∥gm. Area of △ABM = ½×(BC/2)×h = (BC×h)/4. Area ∥gm = BC×h. Ratio = 1:4.

Set CChallenge

If diagonals of a quadrilateral bisect each other, prove it is a parallelogram.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Let diagonals bisect at O: AO=OC, BO=OD. In △AOB and △COD: AO=CO, BO=DO, ∠AOB=∠COD (vert. opp.). By SAS: △AOB≅△COD → AB=CD, ∠OAB=∠OCD → AB∥CD. Similarly BC∥AD. So ABCD is a parallelogram. ✓

In ∥gm ABCD, P and Q are midpoints of AB and CD. Prove APCQ is a parallelogram.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Wait: P on AB, Q on CD (midpoints). AP=AB/2=CD/2=CQ (since AB=CD). Also AP∥CQ (since AB∥CD). So APCQ is a parallelogram (one pair of sides equal and parallel). ✓

Show that a diagonal of a parallelogram divides it into two congruent triangles.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: In ∥gm ABCD, diagonal AC: △ABC and △CDA. AB=CD, BC=AD (opp. sides), AC=CA. By SSS: △ABC≅△CDA. ✓ Area of each = half area of ∥gm.

In ∥gm ABCD, E is any point on AB. Prove: area of △BCE = area of △DCE.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Both triangles have base CE (common). Heights from B and D to line CE... actually, use: △BCE and △ADE share same base CE, between parallels BC and AD → equal area? Better: △ABD area = △CBD area (diagonal BD bisects ∥gm). Area △AEB = Area △DEB... use area subtraction argument.

In ∥gm ABCD with diagonals at O. X is midpoint of AO, Y midpoint of CO. Prove BXDY is a parallelogram.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: AO=OC (diagonals bisect). X midpoint AO, Y midpoint OC → XO=OY (each = AO/2). So diagonals XY and BD bisect each other at O. ∴ BXDY is a parallelogram.

The area of ∥gm = base × height. Prove this using a rectangle.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: Cut the triangular end off the parallelogram and attach to the other side → forms a rectangle with same base and height. Area unchanged. Area rectangle = b×h. ∴ Area ∥gm = b×h.

In ∥gm ABCD, P is on diagonal AC such that AP = 1/3 AC. Show triangles APD and CPB are not congruent.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: AP=AC/3, CP=2AC/3. Since AP≠CP, and triangles APD, CPB have different sides (PD≠PB in general) → not congruent unless specific conditions met. They have equal areas (each = 1/6 area ∥gm) but are not congruent.

Show: if both pairs of opposite angles of a quadrilateral are equal, it is a parallelogram.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: ∠A=∠C=α, ∠B=∠D=β. Sum: 2α+2β=360° → α+β=180°. ∠A+∠B=180° → AB∥DC. ∠B+∠C=180° → BC∥AD. Two pairs parallel → parallelogram. ✓

The midpoints of sides of any quadrilateral form a parallelogram (Varignon's theorem). State it.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: If ABCD is any quadrilateral with midpoints P,Q,R,S of sides AB,BC,CD,DA, then PQRS is always a parallelogram. (Each side of PQRS is parallel to and half the length of a diagonal of ABCD.)

In ∥gm ABCD, ∠A = 3∠B. Show that AB = 2/√3 × BC is NOT necessarily true, but find the angle values.
Hard
Show Answer

Answer: ∠A+∠B=180°. 3∠B+∠B=180° → 4∠B=180° → ∠B=45°. ∠A=135°, ∠C=135°, ∠D=45°. Side ratio not determined by angles alone.
